Chunar Fort

Ancient stronghold on the Ganges – from Mauryan to Mughal and British eras

Location & Dynasty

City Mirzapur, Uttar Pradesh, India
Coordinates 25.1039° N, 82.8756° E
Dynasty Multiple (Pre-Mauryan, Sher Shah Suri, Mughal, British)

Historical Background

Chunar Fort’s origins trace back to the pre-Mauryan era, with major expansions by Sher Shah Suri in the 16th century. Its strategic position on the Ganges made it a coveted military and trade center for Mauryas, Mughals, Nawabs of Awadh, and the British. The fort’s control shifted through the hands of Prithviraj Chauhan, Babar, Akbar, and later the British, who used it as a military base until 1947.
References: R.N. Misra, 2016; Wikipedia; Trawell.in

Architecture

  • Sandstone ramparts and towers overlooking the Ganges, built in tiers
  • Blend of Hindu, Afghan, and Mughal styles with minimal ornamentation
  • Key structures: Sonwa Mandap pavilion (28 pillars, Hindu style), mosque (Mughal period), bungalows (British period)
  • Four main gates: Delhi, Varanasi, Khirki, and Rana Darwaza
  • Citadel, underground dungeons, and a deep well (bawdi) with perennial water
References: Harris, 2006; Tourism India

Building Materials & Properties

Material Use Properties
Chunar Sandstone Walls, pavilions, gates Strength 40–60 MPa, 5–8% porosity, durable, carvable
Lime-Surkhi Mortar Binding masonry Hydraulic, breathable, organic additives (jaggery, neem) for durability

References: Ngoma, 2009; CE & CR, 2021

Builder’s Thought Process

Sher Shah Suri and later rulers chose local Chunar sandstone for its strength and availability. Lime-surkhi mortar, with organic additives, provided flexibility and resilience against the Ganges’ humidity, following Manasara treatise principles.
Reference: R.N. Misra, 2016

Structural Design & Current Condition

  • Load-bearing sandstone masonry, thick sloping walls, deep foundations
  • Stable overall, but riverbank erosion is a concern
  • ASI uses lime-based repairs to address weathering and cement-related cracks

Reference: INTACH Heritage at Risk Register

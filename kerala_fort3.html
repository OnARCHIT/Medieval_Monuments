Chandragiri Fort, Kasaragod

#fort #kerala #kasaragod #medieval #civilengineering

Location & Period

Location: Chandragiri, Kasaragod District
Built: 17th century
Builder: Sivappa Nayaka (Keladi Nayaka dynasty)

History

  • Constructed for coastal defense and control of the Chandragiri River.
  • Currently in ruins, but walls and bastions are still visible.

Architecture

  • Irregular shape, following the hill's contour.
  • Bastions, moat (now dry), underground chambers.
  • Materials: Laterite stone, lime mortar.

Civil Engineering & Materials

  • Foundation: Built into bedrock for stability.
  • Walls: Thick laterite blocks, lime mortar.
  • Water Management: Moat (for defense), wells (for freshwater).

Builders’ Thought Process

  • Coastal defense: Designed to withstand naval attacks.
  • Elevated location for surveillance of the river and sea.
  • Self-sufficiency: Wells for freshwater.

Modern Comparison

Feature Traditional (Laterite/Lime) Modern RCC/Steel
Durability 350+ years 80–120 years
Maintenance Low (ruined) Frequent

Unique Features

  • Hilltop location.
  • Ruins still visible.
  • Scenic views of Chandragiri River and Arabian Sea.

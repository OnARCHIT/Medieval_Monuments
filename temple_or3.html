Lingaraj Temple, Bhubaneswar

Pinnacle of Kalinga Architecture, Odisha, India

Location

  • City: Bhubaneswar, Khurda District, Odisha, India
  • Coordinates: 20.2384° N, 85.8335° E

Dynasty

  • Somavanshi Dynasty (11th century CE)

Deity

  • Shiva

Historical Background

Built in the 11th century, the Lingaraj Temple is a pinnacle of Kalinga architecture, dedicated to Lord Shiva. It epitomizes Bhubaneswar’s status as a temple city and is the largest temple in the city.
Source: Wikipedia

Building Materials & Properties

  • Khondalite Stone: ~50–70 MPa strength, ~5–10% porosity
  • Lime Mortar: Non-hydraulic, binder:aggregate ratio ~1:3, with organic additives
  • Properties: Khondalite is durable, ideal for carvings; lime mortar is breathable, suited for humid climates

Comparison: Lime Masonry vs RCC

  • Lingaraj Temple: Flexible, breathable, suited to humid conditions
  • RCC Structures: Stronger but less breathable, prone to cracking
  • Advantage: Lime mortar’s porosity prevents moisture damage
Unique Features:
  • Natural Shivalingam: Self-manifested lingam, central to worship
  • Torana Carvings: Ornate gateways with intricate sculptures
  • Rekha deula tower (180 ft), 150+ subsidiary shrines, sandalwood entrance door
  • East-facing, four-part axial layout: vimana, jagamohan, nat mandir, bhog mandap

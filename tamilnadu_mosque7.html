Thousand Lights Mosque, Chennai

#mosque #shia #multidome #civilengineering

Location & Builder

Location: Thousand Lights, Anna Salai, Chennai
Built: 1810 CE
Builder: Nawab Umdat ul-Umara (Arcot Nawab)

History

  • Constructed by the Nawabs of Arcot for the Shia community of Chennai[1][2][3][6].
  • Named for the tradition of lighting a thousand oil lamps during special occasions[1][2][5][6].
  • Important site for Shia rituals, especially during Muharram[3][5][6].
  • Has played a pivotal role in the social and religious life of Chennai’s Muslim community[6].

Architecture

  • Multi-domed structure with several minarets (average height 64 feet)[2][3][5][6].
  • Medieval Islamic style, with Quranic verses inscribed on walls and ornate arches[1][2][3][5][6].
  • Large prayer hall, women’s gallery, and two rooms (Bargah) on the second floor as sanctuaries[2][3][5].
  • Facade adorned with intricate calligraphy and decorative elements[3][6].

Civil Engineering & Materials

  • Brick, lime mortar, stucco, and marble used for construction[1][3][5][6].
  • Multiple domes for ventilation, light, and visual grandeur[1][3].
  • Deep foundation to support the weight of domes and minarets[1].

Builders’ Thought Process

  • Multi-domed design for both symbolic and practical reasons (ventilation, light)[1][3].
  • Created to accommodate large gatherings and Shia rituals (azadari, matam)[1][3][5][6].

Unique Features

  • Major Shia mosque in India, site of Muharram processions and matam[1][3][5][6].
  • Quranic calligraphy and tradition of lighting a thousand lamps[1][2][3][5][6].
  • Library, guest house, and burial ground for prominent Shia leaders[2][3][5].

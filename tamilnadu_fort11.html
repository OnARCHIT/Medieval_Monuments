Aranthangi Fort

#fort #tondaiman #mudfort #civilengineering

Location & Era

Location: Pudukkottai District
Built: 16th–17th century
Dynasty: Tondaimans (feudal chiefs under Vijayanagara and Pandyas)

History

  • Built by the Tondaimans, feudal chiefs who ruled the region from the 15th–18th centuries.
  • Served as a local power center, allied with the British against Hyder Ali and Tipu Sultan.
  • The fort’s exact construction date and purpose are unclear; likely built for local defense and as a show of authority.

Architecture

  • Unique for its mud walls: Massive mud embankments, not stone or brick.
  • No surviving palace or major structures inside; only remnants of the mud ramparts.
  • Nearby is a Chola-period Hindu temple (11th c., Rajendra Chola).

Civil Engineering & Materials

  • Materials: Large earthen embankments, compacted mud, no stone or brick.
  • Walls: Wide and tall, designed for rapid construction and local resource use.
  • Water Management: No major tanks or moats documented.

Builders’ Thought Process

  • Used local mud for speed and economy.
  • Focused on defense against cavalry and small arms, not artillery.

Unique Features

  • One of the few surviving mud forts in Tamil Nadu.
  • Adjacent Chola temple provides rare continuity of sacred space.

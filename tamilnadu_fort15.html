Udayagiri Fort

#fort #travancore #granite #civilengineering

Location & Era

Location: Near Nagercoil
Built: 17th century (Venadu king), rebuilt 18th century by Marthanda Varma

History

  • Originally a mud fort, rebuilt with granite by Marthanda Varma in the 18th century.
  • Served as a Dutch cantonment under De Lannoy, a Dutch admiral who became Travancore’s commander.

Architecture

  • Massive granite walls, bastions, covering a 90-acre area.
  • Includes chapel and tomb of De Lannoy, watchtowers, and stables.

Civil Engineering & Materials

  • Materials: Granite blocks, lime mortar.
  • Walls: High, thick, with bastions for artillery.
  • Water Management: Rainwater tanks and moat.

Builders’ Thought Process

  • Granite chosen for strength and durability.
  • Strategic location for defense, adapted to European artillery warfare.

Modern Comparison

Feature Traditional Modern RCC
Wall Strength Granite RCC, less durable

Unique Features

  • De Lannoy’s tomb inside the fort.
  • Now a biodiversity park.

Dhauli Shanti Stupa

Ashokan Edicts & World Peace Stupa, Bhubaneswar, Odisha, India

Location

  • City: Dhauli, Bhubaneswar, Khurda District, Odisha, India
  • Coordinates: 20.1900° N, 85.8400° E

Dynasty

  • Mauryan (3rd century BCE, Ashokan Edicts), Modern (1970s, Stupa)

Deity

  • Buddhist (Commemorates peace and Buddha)

Historical Background

Dhauli is significant for Ashoka’s rock edicts (3rd century BCE) post-Kalinga War, promoting Buddhist principles. The modern Shanti Stupa, built in the 1970s by the Japan Buddha Sangha, symbolizes peace.
Source: Wikipedia

Building Materials & Properties

  • Sandstone (Edicts): ~40–60 MPa strength, ~5–8% porosity
  • Concrete (Stupa): Modern, ~20–30 MPa strength, low porosity
  • No Mortar (Edicts): Rock-cut inscriptions
  • Properties: Sandstone is durable, ideal for inscriptions; concrete is strong, weather-resistant, modern aesthetic

Comparison: Concrete/Rock-Cut vs RCC

  • Dhauli Stupa: Concrete matches RCC’s strength; rock-cut edicts are more permanent
  • RCC Structures: Similar to stupa’s concrete but less durable than rock-cut sandstone
  • Advantage: Rock-cut longevity surpasses RCC
Unique Features:
  • Ashokan Edicts: Promote Buddhist principles post-Kalinga War
  • Modern Stupa: Blends Japanese and Mauryan Buddhist aesthetics
  • Four Buddha statues, lion capitals, panoramic hilltop views

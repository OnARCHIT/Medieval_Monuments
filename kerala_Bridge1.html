Punalur Suspension Bridge, Kollam

#bridge #kerala #kollam #suspension #civilengineering

Location & Era

Location: Punalur, Kollam District
Built: 1877
Designer: Albert Henry (Scottish engineer)
Commissioned by: Travancore King Ayilyam Thirunal Rama Varma

History

  • First motorable suspension bridge in South India, designed to facilitate trade between Kollam and Sengottai and prevent wild animals from entering the town.
  • Now open only for pedestrian traffic; a heritage site and tourist attraction.

Architecture

  • Suspension bridge with massive stone arches and iron chains.
  • 400 feet (120 m) long, 20 feet (6 m) wide.
  • Four deep wells anchor the iron chains; deck made of Kambam wood.
  • Materials: Iron chains, Kambam wood, masonry, stone arches.

Civil Engineering & Materials

  • Foundation: Four deep wells anchoring the iron chains.
  • Superstructure: Iron chains, wooden deck, stone arches.
  • Water Management: Designed to withstand heavy river currents.
  • Ventilation: Open structure for airflow.

Builders’ Thought Process

  • Facilitate trade and ensure safety by preventing wild animal crossings.
  • Innovative use of imported iron and local wood for durability and longevity.
  • First motorable suspension bridge in South India, reflecting technological advancement.

Modern Comparison

Unique Features

  • Only suspension bridge of its kind in South India.
  • Massive stone arches and iron chains.
  • No longer motorable, only pedestrian use.
  • Heritage site and tourist attraction.

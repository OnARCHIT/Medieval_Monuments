Kailasanathar Temple, Kanchipuram

#temple #pallava #sandstone #civilengineering

Location & Dynasty

Location: Kanchipuram
Dynasty: Pallava (8th century)
Deity: Shiva

History

  • Built by Narasimhavarman II (Rajasimha), the oldest structural stone temple in Tamil Nadu.

Architecture

  • Square-plan, pyramidal vimana, 58 shrines on enclosure walls.
  • Sandstone, intricate reliefs, secret tunnel.

Civil Engineering & Materials

  • Sandstone for carving, lime mortar for flexibility.
  • Foundation on compacted earth and stone.

Builders’ Thought Process

  • Experimented with structural stone techniques.
  • Built for spiritual symbolism and artistic innovation.

Modern Comparison

Feature Traditional Modern RCC
Carving Detail High, hand-carved Rare in RCC
Erosion Sandstone erodes faster RCC needs coatings

Unique Features

  • Model for later Dravidian temples.

Hazarduari Palace, Murshidabad

Mughal-Colonial Marvel, West Bengal, India

Location

  • City: Murshidabad, West Bengal, India

Dynasty

  • Nawabs of Bengal (1837 CE)

Deity

  • None (Palace)

Historical Background

Built in 1837 by the Nawabs under Duncan Macleod, Hazarduari Palace reflects Mughal decline and British influence. Now an ASI museum, it showcases the Nawabs’ legacy.
Source: Wikipedia

Building Materials & Properties

  • Brick: Fired for durability
  • Marble (Limited): Decorative flooring
  • Lime Mortar: Binds bricks, resists humidity
  • Iron: Used sparingly, reflects colonial engineering

Comparison: Brick/Lime vs RCC

  • Hazarduari Palace: Brick lacks RCC’s tensile strength, needs more columns
  • RCC Structures: Enable taller, thinner forms
  • Advantage: Palace materials provide thermal insulation, suit local climate
Unique Features:
  • Thousand Doors: Enhance security and aesthetics
  • Mughal-Colonial Fusion: Domes, neoclassical columns, gardens
  • Museum Role: Preserves Nawabi artifacts
  • Grand dome, Corinthian columns, symmetrical layout

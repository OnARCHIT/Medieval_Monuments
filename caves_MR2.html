Historical Background

  • Location: Ellora, Aurangabad, Maharashtra, India (20.0260° N, 75.1790° E)
  • Dynasty: Rashtrakuta (6th–10th century CE)
  • Deity: Buddhist, Hindu, Jain (Multiple deities)
  • UNESCO World Heritage Site: Since 1983
“Ellora Caves are a testament to India’s religious harmony, with Buddhist, Hindu, and Jain monuments carved into basalt cliffs.”
– UNESCO

Architecture

  • Plan: 34 caves: Buddhist viharas, Hindu temples (notably Kailasa), Jain shrines
  • Material: Carved into basalt cliffs, monolithic structures
  • Features: Kailasa Temple (Cave 16) is the world’s largest monolithic rock-cut temple
  • Style: Combines Dravidian, Nagara, and regional influences
  • Spatial Harmony: Layout reflects Manasara principles

Building Materials & Properties

Material Properties Reason for Use
Basalt Compressive strength 100–150 MPa, <2% porosity, durable Ideal for large-scale monolithic carvings
Lime Plaster Breathable, protects carved surfaces Used for minor finishes and preservation

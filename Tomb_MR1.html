Mini Taj Mahal, Aurangabad, Maharashtra, India

Location

  • Coordinates: 19.9014° N, 75.3203° E

Dynasty

Deity

  • None (Mausoleum for Dilras Banu Begum)

Historical Background

Commissioned in 1660 by Aurangzeb for his wife Dilras Banu Begum, Bibi Ka Maqbara, designed by human architects, is known as the “Mini Taj Mahal” for its Mughal resemblance. It reflects human craftsmanship in Mughal architecture.
Source: Holidify

Building Materials & Properties

  • Marble: Limited use for central tomb (~60–80 MPa strength, <1% porosity)
  • Plaster: Stucco over brick core (~5–10 MPa strength)
  • Brick: Core structure (~10–20 MPa)
  • Lime-Surkhi Mortar: Binder:aggregate ratio ~1:2, with organic additives
  • Properties: Marble is durable and aesthetic but costly; plaster is lightweight and mimics marble

Comparison: Lime Masonry vs RCC

  • Bibi Ka Maqbara: Flexible, breathable, suited to semi-arid climates
  • RCC Structures: Stronger but less breathable, prone to cracking
  • Advantage: Lime mortar’s porosity prevents moisture damage
Unique Features:
  • Mini Taj Mahal: Resemblance to Taj Mahal at reduced scale
  • Charbagh Garden: Mughal-style quadrilateral garden layout
  • Intricate plaster and marble ornamentation

Historical Background

Built by Raja Bir Singh Deo in 1613 atop Bangra hill, Jhansi Fort was a strategic Bundela stronghold. It later played a pivotal role in the 1857 uprising, famously defended by Rani Lakshmibai. The fort’s design follows Rajput military architecture, with later Maratha and British modifications.
References: R.N. Misra, 2016; Wikipedia

Architecture & Layout

  • 10 main gates: Khanderao, Datia, Bhanderi, Unnao, BadaGaon, Laxmi, Sagar, Orchha, Saiyar, Chand
  • Granite walls 16–20 m high, 22 bastions, deep moat on two sides
  • Kadak Bijli cannon platform and Baradari pavilion as key features
  • Complex covers 15 acres (312m × 225m)
References: Harris, 2006; District Jhansi

References: Ngoma, 2009; CE & CR, 2021

Builder’s Thought Process

Raja Bir Singh Deo prioritized defense, using locally sourced granite for strength and durability. Lime mortar with jaggery and rice husk provided flexibility and resilience in the region’s semi-arid climate, following Manasara treatise principles.
Reference: R.N. Misra, 2016

Structural Design & Current Condition

  • Load-bearing granite masonry, thick sloped walls, deep foundations
  • Stable overall; some weathering on sandstone; ASI uses lime-based repairs
  • Moat and bastions enhance defense and stability

Reference: INTACH Heritage at Risk Register

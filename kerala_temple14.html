Sree Padmanabhaswamy Temple, Thiruvananthapuram

#temple #kerala #thiruvananthapuram #medieval #civilengineering

Location & Period

Location: Thiruvananthapuram, Thiruvananthapuram District
Period: 16th–18th century (major medieval-era expansions)
Deity: Vishnu

History

  • One of the most famous temples in Kerala, with major renovations by Marthanda Varma in the 18th century.
  • Known for its immense wealth and strict entry rules.

Architecture

  • Srikovil: Main shrine with a reclining Vishnu idol.
  • Nalambalam: Quadrangular enclosure.
  • Namaskara Mandapam: Detached hall for rituals.
  • Prakara: Massive enclosure with shrines for sub-deities, temple tank, and ornate corridors.
  • Gopuram: Tall, ornate entrance tower in Dravidian style.

Civil Engineering & Materials

  • Foundation: Granite.
  • Superstructure: Laterite, wood, and clay tiles.
  • Roof: Sloping, tiled, timber frame.
  • Water Management: Temple tank and wells.

Builders’ Thought Process

  • Sloping roofs and raised platforms for climate adaptation.
  • Proportionate design based on the garbhagriha module.
  • Security focus with massive walls and strict entry controls.

Modern Comparison

Feature Traditional (Wood/Laterite) Modern RCC/Steel
Durability 500+ years 80–120 years
Maintenance Moderate Frequent

Unique Features

  • Reclining Vishnu: Unique idol posture.
  • Immense wealth: Famous for its treasure vaults.
  • Strict entry rules: Only Hindus allowed inside the inner sanctum.

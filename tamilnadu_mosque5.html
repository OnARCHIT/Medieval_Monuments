Palaiya Jumma Palli (Old Jumma Masjid), Kilakarai

#mosque #earlyislam #tamilislam #dravidian #civilengineering

Location & Era

Location: Kilakarai, Ramanathapuram District
Built: 628–630 CE (reconstructed 1036 CE)
Architect: Bazan Ibn Sasan

History

  • One of the oldest mosques in India and the world, built during the lifetime of Prophet Muhammad[1][2][3][4][5][6].
  • Founded by Arab traders and local converts in the ancient port town of Kilakarai.
  • Reconstructed in 1036 CE; remains an active mosque and a symbol of Tamil-Islamic architecture.

Architecture

  • Style: Tamil Islamic, blending Dravidian temple traditions with Islamic requirements (no idols, no ornate sculpture).
  • Plan: Rectangular prayer hall, one dome, two minarets.
  • Materials: Coral stone, lime mortar, wooden beams—using local temple-building techniques.
  • No sculpted images, in keeping with Islamic aniconism.
  • Courtyard for community gatherings.

Civil Engineering & Materials

  • Coral stone chosen for abundance, lightness, and ability to regulate humidity in a coastal climate.
  • Lime mortar is flexible and self-healing, suited for salt-laden air.
  • Foundation built on compacted sand and coral, resilient to coastal erosion.
  • Roof uses timber beams and tiles, reflecting Dravidian influence.

Builders’ Thought Process

  • Used local artisans familiar with Dravidian temple construction, but omitted all idol imagery.
  • Focused on community needs: simple, open, and oriented toward Mecca.
  • Chose coral and lime for resilience to sea air and easy availability.

Unique Features

  • Among the first mosques in India and the oldest in Tamil Nadu.
  • A living example of Tamil-Islamic architectural fusion.
  • Still an active place of worship and community center.

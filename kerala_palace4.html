Kanakakkunnu Palace, Thiruvananthapuram

#palace #kerala #thiruvananthapuram #colonial #civilengineering

Location & Era

Location: Thiruvananthapuram, Thiruvananthapuram District
Built: 19th century (during the reign of Sree Moolam Thirunal)

History

  • Constructed during the late 19th century under Sree Moolam Thirunal[1].
  • Originally used for royal banquets and cultural events[1].
  • Currently owned by the Kerala Government and serves as a venue for cultural programs and events[1].

Architecture

  • Kerala-style façade with colonial influences[1].
  • Large halls, open-air auditorium, and tennis courts[1].
  • Materials: Brick, wood, lime mortar, clay tiles[1].

Civil Engineering & Materials

  • Foundation: Raised plinth for flood protection[1].
  • Walls: Brick, lime mortar[1].
  • Roof: Timber truss, tiled for drainage[1].
  • Water Management: Rainwater harvesting, wells[1].
  • Ventilation: High ceilings, open corridors[1].

Builders’ Thought Process

  • Designed for royal entertainment—banquets and cultural events[1].
  • Adapted to Kerala’s climate: high ceilings, cross-ventilation, tiled roof[1].
  • Included modern amenities for its time: tennis courts, auditoriums[1].

Modern Comparison

Feature Traditional (Brick/Wood) Modern RCC/Steel
Durability 100+ years (with upkeep) 80–120 years
Maintenance Moderate Frequent

Unique Features

  • Kerala-style façade with colonial influences[1].
  • Venue for cultural programs and events[1].

Sarnath Stupa (Dhamek Stupa)

Commemorating Buddha’s First Sermon – Mauryan, Gupta & Medieval Heritage

Location & Dynasty

City Varanasi, Uttar Pradesh, India
Coordinates 25.3811° N, 83.0246° E
Dynasty Mauryan Empire (3rd century BCE, Gupta & medieval additions)

Historical Background

Built in the 3rd century BCE by Emperor Ashoka, the Dhamek Stupa at Sarnath was expanded during the Gupta period (4th–6th century CE) and medieval times. It marks the site of Buddha’s first sermon (Dharma Chakra Pravartana) and is a key Buddhist pilgrimage destination.
References: Eaton, 2001; Wikipedia; Guide Varanasi

Architecture

  • Cylindrical shape: 28 m diameter, 43.6 m high (current form)
  • Solid brick core with Chunar sandstone cladding
  • Lower part covered in carved stone with floral, geometric, and human/bird motifs (Gupta craftsmanship)
  • Eight niches facing eight directions, likely for Buddha images
  • Unfinished upper part, base possibly from Ashokan period
References: R.N. Misra, 2016; Trawell.in

Building Materials & Properties

Material Use Properties
Brick Core structure Locally sourced, high porosity, ~10–20 MPa strength
Chunar Sandstone Cladding, carvings 40–60 MPa strength, durable, carvable, fine grain
Lime Mortar Binding masonry Non-hydraulic, breathable, moderate strength (5–10 MPa), organic additives (rice husk, jaggery)

References: Ngoma, 2009; CE & CR, 2021; Cultural India

Builder’s Thought Process

Ashoka and later Gupta builders aimed to create a sacred monument using brick for solidity and sandstone for aesthetics. Lime mortar with organic additives ensured durability in Varanasi’s humid climate, following Manasara principles.
Reference: R.N. Misra, 2016

Structural Design & Current Condition

  • Solid brick core with sandstone cladding, no arches
  • Stable, with minor weathering; ASI uses lime-based repairs
  • Eight-sided niches and unfinished upper part

Reference: INTACH Heritage at Risk Register

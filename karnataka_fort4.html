Jamalabad Fort

#fort #karnataka #dakshina_kannada #tipu #civilengineering

Location & Era

Location: Beltangady, Dakshina Kannada District
Built: 1794 (by Tipu Sultan)

History

  • Constructed by Tipu Sultan, named after his mother Jamalabee.
  • Used as a military outpost and hill fort for surveillance and defense.
  • Currently a protected monument, managed by the Karnataka State Department of Archaeology.

Architecture

  • Hill fort style, with stone fortifications.
  • Features stone walls, bastions, watchtowers, and water tanks.
  • Materials: Stone, lime mortar.

Civil Engineering & Materials

  • Foundation: Built into the hilltop for natural defense.
  • Walls: Stone blocks, lime mortar.
  • Water Management: Rock-cut water tanks for self-sufficiency during sieges.
  • Defense: Bastions, watchtowers, and narrow gateways for security.

Builders’ Thought Process

  • Military focus: Designed for surveillance and defense.
  • Self-sufficiency: Water tanks for sieges.
  • Adaptation: Built into the hilltop for added defense.

Modern Comparison

Feature Traditional (Stone/Lime) Modern RCC/Steel
Durability 200+ years (with upkeep) 80–120 years
Maintenance Moderate Frequent

Unique Features

  • Named after Tipu Sultan’s mother.
  • Hilltop location for surveillance and defense.
  • Protected monument, open to visitors.

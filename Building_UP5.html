Bara Imambara

Lucknow’s Grand Shia Congregation Hall and Architectural Wonder

Location & Dynasty

City Lucknow, Uttar Pradesh, India
Coordinates 26.8690° N, 80.9129° E
Dynasty Awadh Nawabs (1784 CE)

Historical Background

Commissioned by Nawab Asaf-ud-Daula in 1784 as a famine relief project, Bara Imambara (also known as Asafi Imambara) is a grand Shia congregation hall and one of Lucknow’s most iconic monuments. It provided employment for thousands during a devastating famine and is the world’s second largest imambara.
References: Shamsuzzoha & Islam, 2011; Wikipedia

Architecture

  • Central vaulted hall: 50 m long, 15 m high, unsupported by beams or girders
  • Bhulbhulaiya (labyrinth): 3D maze with 489 interconnected doorways
  • Asfi Mosque and Bowli (stepwell) included in the complex
  • Designed by Kifayatullah, who is buried in the main hall alongside Asaf-ud-Daula
  • Blend of Mughal, Awadhi, and Persian architectural styles
References: Tappin, 2003; UP Tourism

Building Materials & Properties

Material Use Properties
Lakhauri Brick Vaults, walls, labyrinth Thin, flat, high porosity, suited for plastering (~10–20 MPa strength)

References: Ngoma, 2009; CE & CR, 2021; Lucknow Portal

Builder’s Thought Process

Asaf-ud-Daula aimed for a multifunctional religious and cultural space, employing affordable Lakhauri bricks and lime-surkhi mortar to suit Lucknow’s humid climate. The unsupported vaulted roof and labyrinth reflect innovative engineering, inspired by Manasara treatise principles.
Reference: Misra, 2016

Structural Design & Current Condition

  • Load-bearing brick masonry with massive arched roof and vaults
  • Bhulbhulaiya passages enhance structural stability
  • Stable overall; some plaster deterioration addressed by ASI with lime-based repairs

Reference: INTACH Heritage at Risk Register

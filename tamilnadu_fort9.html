Fort St. George, Chennai

#fort #british #chennai #civilengineering

Location & Era

Location: Chennai
Built: 1640 CE
Dynasty: British East India Company

History

  • First English fortress in India, foundation of Madras (now Chennai).
  • Served as the British administrative and military headquarters.

Architecture

  • Tallest flagstaff in India, black Charnockite columns.
  • Star-shaped bastions, thick walls, military barracks, St. Mary’s Church (oldest Anglican church in India).

Civil Engineering & Materials

  • Materials: Charnockite stone, brick, lime mortar.
  • Walls: Thick, sloped for cannon resistance.
  • Water Management: Rainwater drains, moat (now filled in).
  • Foundation: Built on the coast, required deep footings for stability.

Builders’ Thought Process

  • Charnockite for strength and local availability.
  • Designed for rapid defense against rival European powers.

Modern Comparison

Feature Traditional Modern RCC
Wall Strength Thick stone RCC, less mass
Historic Value High Modern, functional

Unique Features

  • Still houses Tamil Nadu’s legislative assembly.
  • St. Mary’s Church, museum with colonial artifacts.

Mehrangarh Fort

Rathore Stronghold, Jodhpur, Rajasthan, India

Location

  • City: Jodhpur, Jodhpur District, Rajasthan, India
  • Coordinates: 26.2980° N, 73.0187° E

Dynasty

  • Rathore Rajputs (15th century CE)

Deity

  • None (Fort-palace)

Historical Background

Founded in 1459 by Rao Jodha, Mehrangarh Fort is a Rathore stronghold, expanded over centuries. Perched on a 125-meter hill, it symbolizes Rajput valor and architectural prowess.
Source: Wikipedia

Building Materials & Properties

  • Sandstone: Chittar sandstone (~40–60 MPa strength, ~5–8% porosity)
  • Lime-Surkhi Mortar: Binder:aggregate ratio ~1:2, with organic additives
  • Brick: Secondary structures (~10–20 MPa)
  • Properties: Sandstone is durable, suited for arid climates; lime-surkhi mortar is hydraulic, breathable

Comparison: Lime Masonry vs RCC

  • Mehrangarh Fort: Flexible, suited to arid climates
  • RCC Structures: Stronger but less breathable, prone to thermal expansion
  • Advantage: Lime mortar’s elasticity absorbs stress
Unique Features:
  • Phool Mahal: Ornate interiors with gold leaf work, a human-crafted luxury
  • Hilltop Bastions: Seven gates for layered defense
  • Massive 21-meter-thick walls, panoramic city views
  • Intricate jali work, palatial courtyards, and museum

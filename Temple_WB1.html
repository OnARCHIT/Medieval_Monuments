Historical Background

  • Location: Kolkata, West Bengal, India
  • Founded: 1855 by Rani Rasmoni
  • Deity: Kali
  • Style: Navaratna (nine spires), Bengali vernacular with colonial influences
“Dakshineswar Kali Temple symbolizes Bengal’s spiritual and cultural resurgence during the colonial era.”

Architecture

  • Style: Navaratna (nine spires), three-storeyed
  • Platform: High, flood-resistant base
  • Sanctum: Square plan with vaulted roof
  • Arches: Multifoiled, for load distribution
  • Shiva Temples: 12 subsidiary shrines
  • Climatic Adaptation: Designed for monsoon conditions

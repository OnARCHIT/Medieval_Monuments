Shore Temple, Mahabalipuram

#temple#pallava#granite#UNESCO#civilengineering

Location & Dynasty

Location: Mahabalipuram
Dynasty: Pallava (8th c.)
Deity: Shiva & Vishnu

History

  • Built by Narasimhavarman II (Rajasimha), Pallava dynasty.
  • UNESCO World Heritage site, part of Mahabalipuram group.

Architecture

  • Granite construction, two main shrines (Shiva and Vishnu).
  • Pyramidal tower, pillared halls, facing Bay of Bengal.
  • Prototype for later Dravidian temples.

Civil Engineering & Materials

  • Finely cut granite blocks, wave-deflecting plinth.
  • Built on bedrock for coastal resilience.
  • Drainage channels to protect against sea water.

Builders’ Thought Process

  • Granite for sea spray resistance, spiritual symbolism.
  • Designed for maritime visibility and ritual use.

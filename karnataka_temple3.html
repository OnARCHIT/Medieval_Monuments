Keshava Temple, Somnathpur

#temple #karnataka #somnathpur #hoysala #civilengineering

Location & Era

Location: Somnathpur, Mandya District
Built: 1268 CE (Hoysala Empire)
Denomination: Vaishnava (Vishnu)

History

  • Built by Somanatha Dandanayaka, a general of Hoysala King Narasimha III.
  • Masterpiece of Hoysala architecture.
  • Dedicated to Lord Keshava (Vishnu), known for its symmetry and carvings.

Architecture

  • Hoysala style, star-shaped plan, intricate carvings.
  • Three shrines (trikuta), 64 cells with carvings, scenes from epics.
  • Materials: Soapstone (chloritic schist), lime mortar.

Civil Engineering & Materials

  • Foundation: Elevated platform (jagati) for drainage and grandeur.
  • Walls: Soapstone blocks, intricate carvings.
  • Roof: Stone slabs, pillared halls for support.
  • Water Management: Temple tank for ritual bathing.

Builders’ Thought Process

  • Artistic expression: Designed to showcase Hoysala artistry.
  • Religious symbolism: Star-shaped plan for cosmic alignment.
  • Community focus: Large halls for gatherings and festivals.

Modern Comparison

Feature Traditional (Stone) Modern RCC/Steel
Durability 750+ years 80–120 years
Maintenance Moderate Frequent

Unique Features

  • Three shrines (trikuta).
  • 64 cells with intricate carvings.
  • UNESCO World Heritage tentative list.

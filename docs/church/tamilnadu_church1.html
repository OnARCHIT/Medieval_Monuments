St. Mary’s Church, Fort St. George, Chennai

#church #anglican #british #civilengineering #colonial

Location & Era

Location: Fort St. George, Chennai
Built: 1678–80 (British East India Company)
Denomination: Anglican (Church of South India)

History

  • Oldest Anglican church in India and the oldest British building in India.
  • Consecrated in 1680; called the “Westminster Abbey of the East.”
  • Site of important colonial marriages and burials (e.g., Robert Clive, Elihu Yale).

Architecture

  • Rectangular nave, bomb-proof roof (4ft thick), thick outer walls (4ft).
  • Nave, aisles, gallery, two curved staircases.
  • Simple, elegant colonial style with minimal ornamentation.

Civil Engineering & Materials

  • Brick and lime mortar, bomb-proof roof for protection during sieges.
  • Deep foundations for stability on coastal soil.

Builders’ Thought Process

  • Designed for defense as well as worship (roof and walls could withstand cannon fire).
  • Used local brick and lime for rapid construction.

Modern Comparison

Feature Traditional (Brick/Lime) Modern RCC
Defense Bomb-proof roof Not designed for war

Unique Features

  • Oldest British tombstones in India, 1660 Bible, silver plates.
  • Still in use for worship and heritage tourism.

St. Stephen’s Church, Ooty

#church #anglican #british #hillstation #civilengineering

Location & Era

Location: Ooty, Nilgiris
Built: 1829–1830 (British)
Denomination: Anglican

History

  • Built by the British for the hill station community.
  • Consecrated in 1830, one of the oldest churches in the Nilgiris.

Architecture

  • Gothic style, wooden beams from Tipu Sultan’s palace.
  • Tall spire, stained glass, large nave.

Civil Engineering & Materials

  • Stone and brick, lime mortar, wooden trusses.
  • Designed for cool, damp climate.

Builders’ Thought Process

  • Used salvaged teak for roof beams.
  • Designed for British aesthetics and local climate.

Unique Features

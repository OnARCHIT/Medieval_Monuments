C.S.I St. Andrew’s Church, Bangalore

#church #karnataka #bangalore #gothic #civilengineering

Location & Era

Location: Cubbon Park, Bangalore, Bengaluru Urban District
Built: 1866
Denomination: Church of South India (Presbyterian origins)

History

  • Constructed by Scottish Presbyterians.
  • One of the oldest Protestant churches in Bangalore.
  • Served as a congregational church and center for Protestant faith.

Architecture

  • Gothic style with pointed arches and stained glass.
  • Tall spire, large nave, stained glass windows.
  • Materials: Brick, stone, lime mortar, stained glass.

Civil Engineering & Materials

  • Foundation: Stone and brick for stability.
  • Walls: Brick and stone, lime mortar.
  • Roof: Timber truss, tiled for drainage.
  • Water Management: Open courtyard for rainwater drainage.
  • Ventilation: High ceilings, stained glass windows for natural light.

Builders’ Thought Process

  • Community focus: Designed for large congregations.
  • Artistic expression: Stained glass for spiritual and aesthetic impact.
  • Climate adaptation: High ceilings and open spaces for ventilation.

Modern Comparison

Feature Traditional (Brick/Stone) Modern (RCC/Steel)
Durability 100+ years (with upkeep) 80–120 years
Maintenance Moderate Frequent

Unique Features

  • Gothic architecture, single tall spire.
  • Stained glass windows.
  • Historical connection to the colonial era.

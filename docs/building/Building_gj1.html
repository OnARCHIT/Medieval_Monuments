Rani ki Vav – The Queen’s Stepwell

Patan, Gujarat, India • UNESCO World Heritage Site

Overview Architecture Materials Gallery Comparison

Historical Background

  • Location: Patan, Gujarat, India (23.8589° N, 72.1017° E)
  • Dynasty: Solanki (Chaulukya), 11th century CE
  • Built by: Queen Udayamati for King Bhimdev I
  • Purpose: Stepwell dedicated to water conservation; memorial to the king
  • UNESCO World Heritage Site: Since 2014
  • Style: Maru-Gurjara (Solanki) architecture
“Rani ki Vav is an exceptional example of a distinctive form of subterranean water architecture, designed as an inverted temple highlighting the sanctity of water.”
– UNESCO

Architecture

  • Size: 64–65 m long, 20 m wide, 27–28 m deep
  • Design: Seven levels of stairs, inverted temple form
  • Sculptures: Over 500 principal, 1000+ minor; mostly Vishnu avatars, apsaras, and geometric motifs
  • Pillars: 212 intricately carved stone columns
  • Water Tank: Rectangular, 9.5 x 9.4 m at 23 m depth
  • Orientation: East–West, with well at the western end

Building Materials & Properties

Material Properties Reason for Use
Sandstone Compressive strength 40–60 MPa, 5–8% porosity, durable, carvable Ideal for intricate sculptures and long-term stability
Lime Mortar Non-hydraulic, breathable, binder:aggregate ~1:3, organic additives (jaggery, rice husk) Flexibility, moisture regulation, climate suitability

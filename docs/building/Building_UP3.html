Dynasty: British Era

Historical Background

Designed by Richard Roskell Bayne, this library is a prime example of Scottish Baronial Revival architecture and is among Uttar Pradesh's largest libraries by size.

Architectural Design

  • Tall towers and arcaded cloisters, with structural polychromy, pointed pillars, and turrets
  • Entrance gate combines triangular stones and arches, enhancing architectural grandeur
  • Decorated stonework from ground to roof; rectangular ground and first floors with lofty openings
  • Main building features huge columns and arches; verandah built with symmetrical construction
  • Highly decorated stones on outer roof connections and columns

Stability Features

  • Main load-bearing elements: rectangular and circular columns, stone-assembled walls
  • Main roof: triangular, supported by wood and iron
  • Dressed stone used for columns, arches, walls, and facades
  • Thin mortar used in stone assembly; arches and rectangular columns provide stability

RCC Comparison

  • Minor cracks in load-bearing stone elements; major cracks in outer decorative elements

Building Materials

  • Granite and sandstone
  • Wood and iron
  • Yellow stone

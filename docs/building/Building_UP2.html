Dynasty: Medieval era under Mughal governance

Historical Background

Constructed in 1887, the All Saints Cathedral Church is a notable example of Anglican architecture in Asia. It perfectly captures the Gothic style of the thirteenth century and represents India's imperial architectural history.

Architecture

  • Two towers originally intended for the main entrance front
  • Dimensions: 40 feet broad and 130 feet long; internal width approximately 56 feet due to narrow nave
  • Features transepts on north and south sides, ambulatory chancel, central tower at nave-transept intersection, and west porch
  • Open verandah and ambulatory designed to hold 300–400 people; overall length extended to 240 feet with nave and western towers
  • Low-set windows designed for extreme heat, can be covered with wet cuscus mats in summer
  • Two carriage porches beneath the towers
  • Peripheral walls and columns on outer arches form broad verandahs on ground, first, and second floors

Building Materials

  • Nave roof constructed with stone masonry and a thin layer of mortar
  • Marble altar, stained glass windows, and red native tiles
  • Built of cream-coloured stone with exquisite red sandstone dressings

Stability Features

  • Arches support the roof due to white ant menace and limited labor skill
  • Load-bearing structural components with large pillars and surrounding arches inside and outside
  • Internal arches run continuously from bottom to top; outer arches vary in size
  • Stone walls show large and small fissures

RCC Comparison

  • Small, insignificant cracks observed on stone columns, arches, and façade walls
  • External color has darkened due to weathering
  • Building and design materials have degraded, warping structural and decorative elements

Reason for Material Selection

Building materials were chosen based on longevity, defensive capability, and cost (materials and time). Base materials formed the bulk of the project. Straw, though lightweight, was seldom found archaeologically in medieval structures.

Anand Bhawan

Indian Government, 1930 — Prayagraj, Uttar Pradesh

About Anand Bhawan

Anand Bhawan is a historic mansion and museum in Prayagraj, built in 1930 by Motilal Nehru. It served as the residence of the Nehru-Gandhi family and was a focal point for India's freedom movement. Today, it stands as a museum preserving the legacy of the Nehrus and the Indian independence struggle.

Building Materials

  • Brick, stone, wood, iron, lead, copper nails
  • Clay and brick for construction and manufacturing
  • Lime mortar (from limestone and oyster shells)
  • Purbeck marble (in select cathedrals)

Material Properties

  • Lime powder provides great thermal protection and binding for stone slabs.
  • Oyster shells used in lime kilns for calcium carbonate.
  • Stone: durable, strong, enables tall, heavy structures, and modular design.
  • Timber frames for strength and endurance.
  • Copper and lead for piping and sheathing; iron rods for structural support.

Structural Design & Engineering

  • Daub and cob construction, fired bricks, and slate roofing (fastened with double nails).
  • Limewash and plaster for weather protection and aesthetics.
  • Balanced layouts, improved tower structures, and fine British-era mortar for durability.
  • Minimal cracks due to robust construction techniques and thick building qualities.

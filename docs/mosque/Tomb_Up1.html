Historical Background

The Khusro Mirza Tomb, built in the Mughal era, is a memorial to Prince Khusro, the son of Emperor Jahangir. Located within the lush gardens of Khusro Bagh, the tomb reflects the grandeur and artistry of Mughal architecture, blending Persian and Indian influences.

Architectural Features

  • Architect: Aqa Reza
  • Southern entrance gate: 60 feet high
  • Garden entry: 18m high archway
  • Multi-domed structure: central dome larger than side domes
  • Open court with octagonal minarets; square and rectangular layouts
  • Main entrance under a half-dome with intricate plaster net-work
  • Prayer chamber: three bays (central square, side rectangular)
  • Platform over 2.5 feet high with vaulted rooms below
  • Corner towers, flanking turrets, and rows of recessed niches

Building Materials & Properties

Material Properties & Usage
Red Sandstone Strong, nonporous, minimal water absorption, visually striking, pollution-induced discoloration
Stone Masonry Random and ashlar forms for walls and foundations
Marble Decorative carvings and inlays
Lime Mortar, Kankar, Surkhi Thick, enhances strength and durability
Gypsum Plaster Finishing, prone to chipping and environmental wear
Mughal Mortar Mix Includes curd, dal, jute, plant gum, jaggery, sugar, glue, straw for strength and durability

Conservation & Structural Challenges

  • Gypsum plaster chipping due to environmental exposure
  • Discoloration from pollutants (SO₃, NO₃, CO₂, fluorides)
  • Cracks and degradation from structural and environmental forces
  • Loss of ornamental ceiling works due to roof leaks
  • Vegetation growth weakening masonry

Barkur Mosque (Tajuddin Mosque), Udupi

#mosque #karnataka #barkur #medieval #civilengineering

Location & Era

Location: Barkur, Udupi District
Built: 645 CE
Denomination: Sunni

History

  • Constructed by followers of Malik Bin Dinar, an early Islamic missionary.
  • Considered the oldest mosque in Karnataka (by some accounts), marking early Islamic presence.
  • Served as a congregational mosque and center for Islamic learning.

Architecture

  • Early Islamic style, with local adaptations.
  • Simple prayer hall, open courtyard, minimal ornamentation.
  • Materials: Stone, lime mortar, wood.
  • Unique elements: Historical inscriptions, ancient well.

Civil Engineering & Materials

  • Foundation: Stone and brick for stability.
  • Walls: Stone blocks, lime mortar.
  • Roof: Tiled, wooden truss.
  • Water Management: Ancient well for ablution.
  • Ventilation: Open courtyard.

Builders’ Thought Process

  • Community focus: Designed for early Muslim settlers and local converts.
  • Cultural integration: Simple, functional design for harmony with local traditions.
  • Climate adaptation: Open courtyard for ventilation.

Modern Comparison

Feature Traditional (Stone/Wood) Modern (RCC/Steel)

Maintenance Moderate Frequent

Unique Features

  • Oldest mosque in Karnataka (by some accounts).

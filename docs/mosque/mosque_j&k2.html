Pathar Masjid, Srinagar

#mosque #mughalarchitecture #stoneconstruction #srinagar #noorjahan

Location & Patron

Location: Zaina Kadal, Srinagar, Jammu & Kashmir
Dynasty/Patron: Mughal Dynasty – Built by Empress Noor Jahan (1623 CE)
Faith: Islam (Shia)

History

  • Commissioned in 1623 CE by Empress Noor Jahan, wife of Emperor Jahangir.
  • Known as "Pathar Masjid" due to its exclusive use of stone in construction.
  • Unlike traditional Kashmiri wooden mosques, it reflects Perso-Mughal architectural style.
  • Was decommissioned from active worship due to political-religious tensions historically.

Architecture

  • Built entirely of grey limestone, hence the name “Pathar” (stone).
  • 9 arches on the façade supported by massive piers.
  • Rectangular plan, with flat wooden ceiling and absence of a central dome.
  • Distinct from traditional mosques in the region which usually used wood and had pagoda-style roofs.

Civil Engineering & Materials

  • Main building material: finely dressed grey limestone masonry.
  • Mortar used for binding stones; thick stone walls with narrow windows.
  • Use of massive arches and horizontal roofing, uncommon in Kashmiri religious architecture.
  • Flat roof laid over timber beams supported by stone brackets.

Materials Properties

Material Properties
Limestone Durable, weather-resistant, easy to shape for large blocks
Timber Flexibility and load distribution for flat roofing
Lime Mortar Breathable, improves over time with carbonation

Reason for Material Selection

  • Limestone was chosen for its imperial appeal and monumental quality.
  • The use of stone over timber was likely meant to emphasize Mughal authority and permanence.
  • Timber beams used in roof construction adapted for local seismic activity and snow load.

Structural Design and Engineering

  • Horizontal load-bearing design with thick stone walls and arched bays.
  • Roof supported by timber beams provided flexibility.
  • Rectangular plan improved internal acoustics and allowed for open prayer space.
  • Deep foundation suited to riverine soil of Zaina Kadal area.

Stability Features

  • Arches distribute lateral loads across the structure.
  • Thick limestone walls provide thermal insulation and durability.
  • Use of timber beams in roofing gives structural flexibility during tremors.
  • Building has stood intact despite centuries of seismic activity and climatic stress.

Builders’ Thought Process

  • Intended as a statement of imperial elegance amidst traditional Kashmiri mosques.
  • Emphasized stone architecture to distinguish it from local wood-based religious buildings.
  • Architecture focused more on formality, durability, and Mughal identity than on regional vernacular.

Unique Features

  • Only Mughal stone mosque in Kashmir Valley.
  • Deviates from the typical multi-tiered wooden Kashmiri mosques.
  • Example of imperial influence in local religious architecture.
  • Often compared to Mughal mosques in Delhi and Agra, but without domes or minarets.

Mosque Inside Vellore Fort

#mosque #arcotnawab #fort #civilengineering

Location & Era

Location: Vellore Fort, Vellore
Built: 1750 CE (Nawab Chanda Sahib / Arcot Nawab period)

History

  • Constructed during the period of the last Arcot Nawab as part of the multi-faith complex inside Vellore Fort[1][3][5][6].
  • Symbolizes the secular and multicultural nature of the fort, which also houses a temple and a church[1][2][5][6].
  • Also known as Nawab Chanda Sahib Mosque; presently not open for prayers due to heritage status[3][5][6].

Architecture

  • Style: Mughal-Arcot/Indo-Saracenic fusion, rectangular prayer hall[1][3][5].
  • Materials: Granite blocks, brick, and lime mortar, matching the fort’s robust construction[1][3][5].
  • Features: Large arched mihrab, minbar, prayer space, central dome with minarets, floral designs[1][3][5].
  • Prayer hall measures 40 feet in length and 15 feet in breadth[3][5].

Civil Engineering & Materials

  • Granite: Used for strength and integration with the fort’s defensive architecture[1][2][3].
  • Foundation: Built atop the fort’s stone platform for stability[1][2].
  • Dome: Plaster and lime finish for waterproofing[3].

Builders’ Thought Process

  • Designed for the spiritual needs of the fort’s Muslim garrison and officials[1].
  • Used durable materials to withstand sieges and the test of time[1][2].

Unique Features

  • Part of India’s most secular fort complex (temple, mosque, church together)[1][2][6].
  • Not always open for worship due to heritage status[1][3][5].

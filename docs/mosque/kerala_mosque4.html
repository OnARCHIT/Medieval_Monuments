Odathil Mosque, Thalassery

#mosque #kerala #kannur #colonial #civilengineering

Location & Era

Location: Thalassery, Kannur District
Built: 1806 CE
Denomination: Sunni

History

  • Built by Moosa Kakka of the Keyi family on land obtained from the Dutch.
  • Significant for its unique temple-style architecture and three-storeyed structure with copper plate roofing.

Architecture

  • Temple-style, three storeys, copper plate roofing, golden finials.
  • Abundant teak wood, countless windows, graveyard for Keyi family only.
  • Materials: Teak wood, copper, laterite, lime mortar.

Civil Engineering & Materials

  • Foundation: Laterite plinth for stability.
  • Walls: Laterite, lime mortar.
  • Roof: Copper plate roofing, wooden truss.
  • Water Management: Natural drainage for monsoon rains.

Builders’ Thought Process

  • Blend of Kerala temple and Islamic architectural traditions for cultural integration.
  • Unique design as a symbol of community identity and pride.

Modern Comparison

Feature Traditional (Wood/Copper) Modern RCC/Steel
Durability 200+ years (with repairs) 80–120 years
Maintenance Moderate Frequent

Unique Features

  • Temple-style architecture.
  • Copper plate roofing and golden finials.

Great Mosque of Dhanbad

Indo-Islamic Heritage of Jharkhand

Historical Background

  • Built during the Mughal period, likely in the 17th century, as Islam spread in the region.
  • Reflects Indo-Islamic architectural influence in Jharkhand’s urban centers.
  • Serves as a religious and community center for Dhanbad’s Muslim population.

Architecture

  • Indo-Islamic style with a large rectangular courtyard, prayer hall, and single dome.
  • Minarets at corners, mihrab (prayer niche) oriented toward Mecca.
  • Ornate calligraphy and geometric patterns inside and outside.

Structural Design

  • Prayer hall with a series of arches supported by slender pillars for a spacious interior.
  • Double-shell dome: inner brick shell, outer stone layer.
  • Slender, tapering minarets designed to withstand wind loads.

Materials Used & Properties

  • Red Sandstone: Facade and minarets, durable and aesthetically pleasing.
  • White Marble: Decorative inlays and calligraphy, weather-resistant.
  • Brick: Core for dome and arches, lightweight and cost-effective.

RCC Comparison

  • RCC domes can span larger areas with less material.
  • Traditional materials are less resistant to seismic activity than RCC.
  • RCC allows faster construction, but traditional materials offer cultural authenticity.
Stability Features:
  • Double-shell dome distributes weight for stability.
  • Arches transfer loads to pillars, reducing wall stress.
  • Courtyard design minimizes wind resistance.
Unique Features:
  • Calligraphy as a decorative hallmark of Indo-Islamic architecture.
  • Orientation toward Mecca integrates religious symbolism.

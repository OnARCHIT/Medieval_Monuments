Masjid Zeenath Baksh, Mangaluru

#mosque #karnataka #mangaluru #dravidian #civilengineering

Location & Era

Location: Bunder, Mangaluru, Dakshina Kannada District
Built: 642/643 CE (AH 22); renovated by Tipu Sultan (18th century)
Denomination: Sunni

History

  • Constructed by the family of Malik Deenar, Arab missionary.
  • Renovated by Tipu Sultan, who named it after his daughter Zeenath Baksh.
  • Third oldest mosque in India, oldest in Karnataka.
  • Served as a congregational mosque and center for Islamic learning.

Architecture

  • Dravidian style, reminiscent of local temple architecture.
  • Wooden inner sanctum with 16 teak pillars, tiled roof, open colonnade, temple tank for ablution.
  • Materials: Teak, rosewood, clay tiles.
  • Unique elements: Life stories of Prophet Muhammad depicted inside, intricate wood carvings.

Civil Engineering & Materials

  • Foundation: Stone plinth for stability.
  • Walls: Wooden panels, rosewood carvings.
  • Roof: Tiled, wooden truss.
  • Water Management: Temple tank for ritual ablution.
  • Ventilation: Open colonnade, high ceilings.

Builders’ Thought Process

  • Cultural integration: Designed to blend with local Dravidian architecture.
  • Community focus: Open spaces for gatherings and learning.
  • Artistic expression: Intricate wood carvings and murals.

Modern Comparison

Feature Traditional (Wood) Modern (RCC/Steel)
Durability 1,300+ years 80–120 years
Maintenance Moderate Frequent

Unique Features

  • Only mosque in Karnataka made entirely of wood.
  • Dravidian architectural style, temple tank for ablution.
  • Life stories of Prophet Muhammad depicted inside.

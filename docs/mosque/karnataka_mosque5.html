Tipu Sultan’s Mosque (Srirangapatna Jama Masjid), Bangalore

#mosque #karnataka #bangalore #tipu #civilengineering

Location & Era

Location: Bangalore, Bengaluru Urban District
Built: 18th century
Denomination: Sunni

History

  • Constructed by Tipu Sultan, ruler of Mysore.
  • One of the oldest mosques in Bangalore, protected monument.
  • Served as a congregational mosque and center for Islamic learning.

Architecture

  • Indo-Islamic style with two minarets and a large prayer hall.
  • Features: Two minarets, large prayer hall, beautiful courtyard.
  • Materials: Stone, brick, lime mortar.
  • Unique elements: Intricate carvings, decorative arches.

Civil Engineering & Materials

  • Foundation: Stone and brick for stability.
  • Walls: Stone blocks, lime mortar.
  • Roof: Domed, supported by arches.
  • Water Management: Open courtyard for rainwater drainage.
  • Ventilation: High ceilings, open arches.

Builders’ Thought Process

  • Community focus: Designed for large congregations and gatherings.
  • Artistic expression: Intricate carvings and decorative arches.
  • Climate adaptation: Open courtyard and high ceilings for ventilation.

Modern Comparison

Feature Traditional (Stone/Brick) Modern (RCC/Steel)

Unique Features

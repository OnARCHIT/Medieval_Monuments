Cheraman Juma Mosque, Kodungallur

#mosque #kerala #thrissur #medieval #civilengineering

Location & Era

Location: Kodungallur, Thrissur District
Built: 629 CE (tradition), current structure from 14th–17th centuries
Denomination: Sunni

History

  • Claimed to be the oldest mosque in India and the oldest in current use on the Indian subcontinent.
  • Legend: Founded by Malik Bin Dinar, a companion of the Prophet Muhammad, in 629 CE.
  • Destroyed by the Portuguese in 1504, rebuilt in the mid-16th to early 17th century.
  • Modern additions: Corridors and halls added in 1984, dome and minarets added in 1994 and removed in 2022 restoration.

Architecture

  • Kerala-Islamic traditional Vastu shastra style, resembling a Kerala tharavad (ancestral house).
  • Wooden doors, tiled roof, hanging lamps, prayer mats, qibla oriented towards Mecca.
  • Materials: Wood, clay tiles, laterite, lime mortar.

Civil Engineering & Materials

  • Foundation: Laterite plinth for stability.
  • Walls: Laterite, lime mortar.
  • Roof: Wooden truss, tiled for drainage.
  • Water Management: Natural drainage for monsoon rains.

Builders’ Thought Process

  • Designed for Kerala’s humid climate.
  • Blend of Kerala and Islamic architectural traditions.
  • Simple, functional design for community worship.

Modern Comparison

Feature Traditional (Wood/Laterite) Modern RCC/Steel
Durability 400+ years (with repairs) 80–120 years
Maintenance Moderate Frequent

Unique Features

  • Oldest mosque in India (by tradition).
  • Wooden doors and tiled roof, no dome or minaret in original design.
  • Ancient oil lamp, said to have burned continuously for over 1,000 years.
  • Restored to original style in 2022.

Orchha Fort

Bundela Rajput Palace-Fortress, Madhya Pradesh, India

Location

  • City: Orchha, Niwari District, Madhya Pradesh, India
  • Coordinates: 25.3500° N, 78.6400° E

Dynasty

  • Bundela Rajputs (16th–17th century CE)

Deity

  • None (Palace-fortress complex)

Historical Background

Built by the Bundela Rajputs in the 16th century, Orchha Fort served as their capital, featuring palaces like Jahangir Mahal and Raja Mahal. It blends Rajput and Mughal architectural styles, reflecting cultural synthesis.
Source: Madhya Pradesh Tourism

Building Materials & Properties

  • Sandstone: Durable, locally sourced, detailed carvings (~40–60 MPa, 5–8% porosity)
  • Lime-Surkhi Mortar: Hydraulic, breathable, organic additives for durability
  • Brick: Used in secondary structures (~10–20 MPa)

Comparison: Lime Masonry vs RCC

  • Lime Masonry: Flexible, breathable, ideal for humid climates
  • RCC: Rigid, strong, but prone to moisture-related cracking
Unique Features:
  • Jahangir Mahal: Symmetrical design, Mughal-inspired chhatris and jali screens
  • Raja Mahal: Frescoes depicting Hindu mythology
  • Phool Bagh: Ingenious water-cooled summer retreat

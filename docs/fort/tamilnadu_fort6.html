Manora Fort, Thanjavur

#fort #maratha #coastal #civilengineering

Location & Builder

Location: Near Pattukkottai, Thanjavur, Tamil Nadu
Built: 1814 CE (by Serfoji II, Maratha)

History

  • Built by Maratha ruler Serfoji II in 1814–1815 to commemorate the British victory over Napoleon Bonaparte at Waterloo.
  • Served as a watchtower, coastal defense, royal residence, and lighthouse.
  • Name "Manora" is derived from "minar" (tower/minaret).

Architecture

  • Eight-storey hexagonal tower, 23 m (75 ft) high, tapering minaret-like design.
  • Surrounded by a wall and moat, with arched windows, circular staircase, and eaves separating each storey.
  • Panoramic views of the Bay of Bengal; looks like a pagoda.

Civil Engineering & Materials

  • Materials: Brick, lime mortar, some timber. Materials were transported by sea from Point Calimere.
  • Foundation: Deep footings on coastal sand to withstand cyclones and erosion.
  • Rampart wall, moat, and safety cellars for defense and storage.

Builders’ Thought Process

  • Designed for visibility, early warning, and coastal defense.
  • Blended Indian and European architectural features, including a Western-style threshold gate.
  • Commissioned Thanjavur palace engineers for innovative design and construction.

Unique Features

  • Only minaret-style fort in Tamil Nadu; combines fort, lighthouse, and residence.
  • Notable for its hexagonal, tapering shape and location right on the Bay of Bengal.

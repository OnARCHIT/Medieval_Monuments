Bekal Fort, Kasaragod

#fort #kerala #kasaragod #medieval #civilengineering

Location & Period

Location: Bekal, Kasaragod District
Period: 17th century (built by Shivappa Nayaka, Keladi Nayaka dynasty)

History

  • Largest and best-preserved fort in Kerala, built by Shivappa Nayaka in the mid-17th century.
  • Later occupied by Hyder Ali, Tipu Sultan, and the British East India Company.
  • Served as a key military outpost and trading center, protecting the Malabar Coast from naval invasions.

Architecture

  • Circular shape with massive laterite walls and bastions.
  • Features an observation tower with panoramic sea views, underground tunnels, moat, and a secret passage to the sea.
  • Water management includes a moat (for defense) and wells for freshwater.
  • Materials: Laterite stone, lime mortar.

Civil Engineering & Materials

  • Foundation: Built into bedrock for stability.
  • Walls: Thick laterite blocks, lime mortar.
  • Observation Tower: Designed for surveillance and signaling.
  • Water Management: Moat (for defense), wells (for freshwater).

Builders’ Thought Process

  • Coastal defense: Designed to withstand naval attacks and sieges.
  • Surveillance: High observation tower for monitoring the Arabian Sea.
  • Self-sufficiency: Wells for freshwater, granaries for food storage.

Modern Comparison

Feature Traditional (Laterite/Lime) Modern RCC/Steel
Durability 350+ years 80–120 years
Maintenance Moderate Frequent

Unique Features

  • Largest fort in Kerala.
  • Observation tower with sea views.
  • Underground tunnels and secret passages.
  • Site of cultural events and festivals.

Amber Fort

Rajput-Mughal Marvel, Jaipur District, Rajasthan, India

Location

  • City: Amber, Jaipur District, Rajasthan, India
  • Coordinates: 26.9855° N, 75.8513° E

Dynasty

  • Kachwaha Rajputs (11th–16th century CE)

Deity

  • None (Fort-palace)

Historical Background

Constructed starting in the 11th century and expanded by Raja Man Singh I in 1592, Amber Fort was the Kachwaha capital until Jaipur’s founding. It blends Rajput and Mughal styles, reflecting human ingenuity in fortification and aesthetics.
Source: Wikipedia

Building Materials & Properties

  • Sandstone: Locally sourced (~40–60 MPa compressive strength, ~5–8% porosity)
  • Marble: Decorative elements (~60–80 MPa strength, <1% porosity)
  • Lime-Surkhi Mortar: Hydraulic, binder:aggregate ratio ~1:2, with organic additives (jaggery, hemp fiber)
  • Properties: Sandstone is durable, ideal for carvings; marble is aesthetic, low porosity; lime-surkhi mortar is breathable, suited for arid climates

Comparison: Lime Masonry vs RCC

  • Amber Fort: Flexible, breathable, suited to arid conditions
  • RCC Structures: Stronger but rigid, prone to cracking
  • Advantage: Lime mortar’s porosity prevents moisture damage
Unique Features:
  • Sheesh Mahal: Mirror-work interiors reflect light, a marvel of human craftsmanship
  • Hilltop Design: Strategic defense with aesthetic integration
  • Four courtyards, ornate gates, and jali screens
  • Blend of Rajput and Mughal architectural elements

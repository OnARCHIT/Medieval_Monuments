Thalassery Fort (Tellicherry Fort), Kannur

#fort #kerala #kannur #colonial #civilengineering

Location & Era

Location: Thalassery, Kannur District
Built: 1708–1718 (British East India Company)

History

  • Constructed by the British East India Company to control trade and defend against local rulers and European rivals[2][3][5][1].
  • Foundation stone laid by the Raja of Kolathunad; land granted to the British in 1708[3].
  • Withstood attacks by local chieftains, Hyder Ali, and Tipu Sultan; played a key role in the Anglo-Mysore Wars[2][5].
  • Served as a military base, administrative center, and trade hub for spices, especially pepper and cardamom[2][5][6][7].
  • Now a well-preserved protected monument and major tourist attraction[1][2][6][7].

Architecture

  • Square (quadrangular) plan with massive laterite walls and bastions at each corner[1][2][4][5][6].
  • Walls about 10 meters high and 3 meters thick, designed to withstand cannon fire[5].
  • Grand arched gateway, intricately carved doors, secret tunnels, and a lighthouse[2][5][6][7].
  • Moat (now dry), barracks, underground chambers, chapel, and commander's residence[1][2][5][6].
  • Materials: Locally abundant laterite stone, lime mortar, wood for doors and upper structures[1][2][5][6].

Civil Engineering & Materials

  • Foundation: Built into bedrock for stability[1][4].
  • Walls: Thick laterite blocks, lime mortar, possibly reinforced with quicklime, egg white, and sugar candy for durability[6][7].
  • Water Management: Moat for defense, wells for freshwater[1][4][6].

Builders’ Thought Process

  • Trade control and defense of British interests[1][2][3][5].
  • Thick walls, bastions, and moat for protection against siege[1][2][4][5].
  • Self-sufficiency: barracks, wells, and storage for supplies[1][2][5].

Unique Features

  • Lighthouse and panoramic sea views[1][6][7].
  • Underground chambers and secret tunnels[1][2][5][6].
  • Site of cultural events and a live classroom for history enthusiasts[1][6].

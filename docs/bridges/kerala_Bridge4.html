Marthanda Varma Bridge (Aluva Bridge), Ernakulam

#bridge #kerala #aluva #modern #civilengineering

Location & Era

Location: Aluva, Ernakulam District
Built: 1940 (old), 2002 (new)

History

  • Old bridge built in 1940, the second arch bridge in Asia.
  • New bridge built in 2002 as a four-lane parallel structure.
  • Connects the banks of the Periyar River and is vital for Aluva’s development.

Architecture

  • Old bridge: Steel arch design.
  • New bridge: Modern concrete construction.
  • Twin bridges with wide carriageway and pedestrian paths.
  • Materials: Steel, concrete.

Civil Engineering & Materials

  • Foundation: Deep piles for river stability.
  • Superstructure: Steel arch (old), pre-stressed concrete (new).
  • Water Management: Designed for monsoon floods.

Builders’ Thought Process

  • Connectivity: Essential for Aluva’s growth.
  • Innovation: Second arch bridge in Asia (old).
  • Safety: Wide lanes and pedestrian paths.

Modern Comparison

Feature Traditional (Steel Arch) Modern (Concrete)
Durability 80+ years (heritage) 50+ years
Maintenance Moderate Moderate

Unique Features

  • Second arch bridge in Asia (old).
  • Twin bridges for increased traffic.
  • Landmark of Aluva, featured in films and literature.

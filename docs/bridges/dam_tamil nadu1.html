Grand Anicut (Kallanai Dam)

#bridge #dam #chola #civilengineering #kaveri

Location & Period

Location: Thanjavur District, across the Kaveri River
Built: 2nd century CE (Chola king Karikalan)
Type: Ancient stone barrage/bridge

History

  • Built around the 2nd century CE, making it the oldest functional water-regulator structure in the world.
  • Constructed by Chola king Karikalan to divert Kaveri river water for irrigation.

Architecture & Civil Engineering

  • Length: 329m; Width: 20m; Height: 5.4m.
  • Materials: Unmortared granite blocks, placed for mass and flow control.
  • Design: Gravity dam, no mortar; blocks interlock by weight.
  • Function: Diverts water into delta channels for irrigation.
  • Modern Upgrades: Reinforced with concrete in the 19th and 20th centuries, but the core is original.

Builders’ Thought Process

  • Used locally quarried granite for strength and availability.
  • Designed for sustainability and minimal maintenance.
  • Created a system that could be repaired or extended as needed.

Modern Comparison

Feature Kallanai (Granite) Modern RCC Dam
Durability 2,000+ years 80–120 years
Maintenance Minimal Regular repairs
Sustainability High (local stone) Lower (cement/steel)

Unique Features

  • Still diverts water for irrigation to millions of acres.
  • UNESCO World Heritage tentative list.
  • Model for sustainable hydraulic engineering.

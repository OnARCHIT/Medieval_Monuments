Thimmankudru Bridge, Udupi

#bridge #karnataka #udupi #pedestrian #civilengineering

Location & Era

Location: Tonse, Udupi District
Built: 1991 (original), upgraded later

History

  • Constructed by NCC cadets as part of training.
  • Connects Tonse village to the mainland.
  • Functional, mainly for pedestrian use.

Architecture

  • Narrow pedestrian bridge, 280 feet long.
  • Originally wooden, now cement slabs.
  • Materials: Cement, steel.

Civil Engineering & Materials

  • Foundation: Concrete piers.
  • Superstructure: Cement slabs, steel supports.
  • Water Management: Elevated for river flow.

Builders’ Thought Process

  • Community connectivity: Designed for isolated villages.
  • Quick construction: Built in 12 days by NCC cadets.
  • Upgraded for durability: Wooden planks replaced with cement.

Modern Comparison

Feature Traditional (Wood) Modern (Cement/Steel)
Durability 30+ years 80–120 years
Maintenance High (wood) Moderate

Unique Features

  • Built in 12 days by NCC cadets.
  • Upgraded from wood to cement.
  • Vital for local connectivity.

Ayamkadavu Bridge, Kasaragod

#bridge #kerala #kasaragod #modern #civilengineering

Location & Era

Location: Periya–Badiadka, Kasaragod District
Built: 2019
Commissioned by: UDF government (foundation laid 2016)
Inaugurated by: Chief Minister Pinarayi Vijayan

History

  • Built to improve connectivity to Sullia, Madikeri, and Central University of Kerala.
  • Vital link for Kasaragod’s rural and academic communities.

Architecture

  • Modern pre-stressed concrete bridge.
  • 120 m long, 25 m high (highest in Kerala), 4 spans of 30 m each, 6 spans of 10 m each.
  • No joints in the entire length; 2.5 m wide footpath.
  • Materials: Pre-stressed concrete.

Civil Engineering & Materials

  • Foundation: Deep piles for stability.
  • Superstructure: Pre-stressed concrete, no expansion joints.
  • Water Management: Designed for monsoon and river flow.

Builders’ Thought Process

  • Focus on connectivity and safety for rural and academic communities.
  • Innovation: No joints for durability and low maintenance.
  • Wide carriageway and footpath for safety.

Modern Comparison

Feature Traditional (Wood/Stone) Modern (Pre-stressed Concrete)
Durability 100+ years (with upkeep) 100+ years
Maintenance Moderate Low

Unique Features

  • Highest bridge in Kerala (25 m).
  • No expansion joints—unique in Kerala.
  • Vital for rural and academic connectivity.

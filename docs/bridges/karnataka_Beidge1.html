Kabni River Bridge, Mysuru

#bridge #karnataka #mysuru #colonial #civilengineering

Location & Era

Location: Mysuru, Mysuru District
Built: 1735 (original), railway tracks added later

History

  • Constructed by Dalvoy Devraj.
  • One of the oldest railway bridges in India, now a heritage monument.
  • No longer used for trains, preserved as a heritage site.

Architecture

  • Gothic style with 51 stone arches.
  • 10-foot arches, stone piers, railway tracks (added later).
  • Materials: Stone, brick, lime mortar.

Civil Engineering & Materials

  • Foundation: Stone piers anchored into riverbed.
  • Walls: Stone and brick arches, lime mortar.
  • Water Management: Arches allow river flow, preventing flooding.

Builders’ Thought Process

  • Trade and transport: Designed for early colonial and princely state needs.
  • Durability: Stone construction for longevity.
  • Heritage value: Preserved as a monument to early engineering.

Modern Comparison

Feature Traditional (Stone/Brick) Modern (Concrete/Steel)
Durability 300+ years (with upkeep) 80–120 years
Maintenance Low (heritage) Frequent

Unique Features

  • Heritage railway bridge, no longer in use.
  • Gothic stone arches, 51 spans.
  • Preserved as a monument.

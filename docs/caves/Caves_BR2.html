Vishwa Shanti Stupa

World Peace Pagoda, Rajgir, Bihar, India

Location

  • City: Rajgir, Nalanda District, Bihar, India
  • Coordinates: 25.0040° N, 85.4200° E

Dynasty

  • Modern (1969, built by Nichiren Buddhist Sect)

Deity

  • Buddhist (Promotes world peace)

Historical Background

Built in 1969 by the Japanese Nichiren Buddhist Sect, the Vishwa Shanti Stupa (World Peace Pagoda) sits atop Ratnagiri Hill (1,000 ft). It houses Buddha’s relics and promotes peace, inspired by Gandhi’s philosophy.
Source: Wikipedia

Building Materials & Properties

  • Concrete: Modern construction (~20–50 MPa strength)
  • Brick: Core structure, plastered (~10–20 MPa)
  • White Plaster: Aesthetic coating, low strength
  • Properties: Concrete is high strength and durable but less breathable; brick is stable and suited for plaster

Comparison: Concrete vs RCC

  • Vishwa Shanti Stupa: Similar to RCC, high strength but less breathable
  • RCC Structures: Comparable, though stupa’s plaster requires maintenance
  • Advantage: Concrete’s durability suits modern design
Unique Features:
  • Golden Buddha statues: Represent Buddha’s life stages
  • Cable car access: Enhances visitor experience
  • Hilltop location: Symbolizes enlightenment and peace

Historical Background

  • Location: Ajanta, Aurangabad, Maharashtra, India (20.5519° N, 75.7003° E)
  • Dynasty: Vakataka (2nd–5th century CE)
  • Deity: Buddhist (Buddha and Bodhisattvas)
  • UNESCO World Heritage Site: Since 1983
“Ajanta Caves are masterpieces of Buddhist rock-cut architecture, famed for their vibrant frescoes and spiritual legacy.”
– UNESCO

Architecture

  • Plan: 30 caves including viharas (monasteries) and chaityas (prayer halls)
  • Material: Carved into basalt cliffs, monolithic structure
  • Features: Stupas, pillars, vaulted ceilings, horseshoe-shaped chaitya arches
  • Murals: Jataka tales, Buddha’s life, Bodhisattvas
  • Spatial Harmony: Layout follows Manasara principles

Building Materials & Properties

Material Properties Reason for Use
Basalt Compressive strength 100–150 MPa, <2% porosity, durable Ideal for rock-cut monolithic architecture
Pigments Mineral-based (ochre, lapis lazuli), fade-resistant Long-lasting frescoes in humid caves

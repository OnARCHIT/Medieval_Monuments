Udayagiri and Khandagiri Caves

Jain Rock-Cut Heritage, Bhubaneswar, Odisha, India

Location

  • City: Bhubaneswar, Khurda District, Odisha, India
  • Coordinates: 20.2630° N, 85.7850° E

Dynasty

  • Kalinga Period (2nd century BCE, Jain patronage)

Deity

  • Jain Tirthankaras

Historical Background

Carved during the 2nd century BCE under King Kharavela, these rock-cut caves served as Jain monastic residences. They reflect early Jain influence in Kalinga, with inscriptions like the Hathigumpha.
Source: Wikipedia

Building Materials & Properties

  • Laterite and Sandstone: ~30–50 MPa strength, ~10–15% porosity, easily carved
  • No Mortar: Rock-cut, no binders used
  • Properties: Laterite is soft when quarried, hardens on exposure; sandstone is durable, suited for reliefs

Comparison: Rock-Cut vs RCC

  • Udayagiri Caves: No mortar, inherently stable due to monolithic structure
  • RCC Structures: Strong but artificial, requiring maintenance against weathering
  • Advantage: Rock-cut permanence outlasts RCC’s lifespan
Unique Features:
  • Hathigumpha Inscription: Chronicles Kharavela’s reign and Jain patronage
  • Jain Reliefs: Early depictions of Tirthankaras and narratives
  • Rani Gumpha: Colossal cave with reliefs of royal life, animals, dancers, musicians
  • Natural ventilation and drainage, 33 caves across twin hills

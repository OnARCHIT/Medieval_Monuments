Barabar Caves

Mauryan Rock-Cut Meditation Caves, Jehanabad, Bihar, India

Location

  • City: Jehanabad, Jehanabad District, Bihar, India
  • Coordinates: 25.0050° N, 85.0630° E

Dynasty

  • Mauryan Empire (323–185 BCE)

Deity

  • Ajivika/Buddhist (Meditation caves)

Historical Background

Carved during the Mauryan period (3rd century BCE), the Barabar Caves are India’s oldest surviving rock-cut caves, used by Ajivika and Buddhist monks. Their polished interiors reflect Mauryan engineering.
Source: Wikipedia

Building Materials & Properties

  • Granite: Locally sourced (~100–150 MPa strength, <1% porosity)
  • No Mortar: Rock-cut monolithic structure
  • Properties: Granite is extremely durable, low porosity, ideal for polishing

Comparison: Rock-Cut vs RCC

  • Barabar Caves: Permanent, no mortar-related issues
  • RCC Structures: Strong but susceptible to weathering
  • Advantage: Granite’s monolithic nature ensures longevity
Unique Features:
  • Polished interiors: Mirror-like finish, rare for 3rd century BCE
  • Acoustic echo: Caves amplify sound, aiding meditation
  • Lomas Rishi Cave: Chaitya arch entrance, precursor to later Buddhist architecture
  • Inscriptions: Ashokan and later, reflecting dynamic religious use

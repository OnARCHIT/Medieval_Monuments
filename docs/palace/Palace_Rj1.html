Hawa Mahal

The Palace of Winds, Jaipur, Rajasthan, India

Location

  • City: Jaipur, Jaipur District, Rajasthan, India
  • Coordinates: 26.9239° N, 75.8267° E

Dynasty

  • Kachwaha Rajputs (1799 CE)

Deity

  • None (Palace facade)

Historical Background

Built in 1799 by Maharaja Sawai Pratap Singh, Hawa Mahal is a five-story sandstone facade designed for royal women to observe street festivities. It exemplifies Rajput-Mughal architectural synthesis.
Source: Wikipedia

Building Materials & Properties

  • Pink Sandstone: Jaipur sandstone (~40–60 MPa strength, ~5–8% porosity)
  • Lime Mortar: Non-hydraulic, binder:aggregate ratio ~1:3, with organic additives
  • Properties: Sandstone is durable, ideal for intricate jali work; lime mortar is breathable, suited for arid climates

Comparison: Lime Masonry vs RCC

  • Hawa Mahal: Flexible, breathable, ideal for arid climates
  • RCC Structures: Stronger but rigid, less suited for intricate facades
  • Advantage: Lime mortar’s porosity prevents cracking
Unique Features:
  • Jali Windows: 953 windows for ventilation and privacy, a human innovation
  • Honeycomb Facade: Resembles Krishna’s crown, symbolic and aesthetic
  • Curvilinear roof, thin walls, and panoramic city views
  • Five-story elevation, Rajput-Mughal blend

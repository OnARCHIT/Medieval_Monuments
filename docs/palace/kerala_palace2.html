Mattancherry Palace (Dutch Palace), Kochi

#palace #kerala #kochi #colonial #civilengineering

Location & Era

Location: Mattancherry, Kochi, Ernakulam District
Built: 1555 CE (Portuguese), renovated by Dutch (1663)

History

  • Built by the Portuguese in 1555 as a gift to the Cochin king.
  • Renovated by the Dutch in 1663 after damage.
  • Served as the royal residence of the Cochin Rajas.
  • Now a museum showcasing portraits, murals, and royal artifacts.

Architecture

  • Traditional Kerala Nallukettu (quadrangular courtyard) with European influences.
  • Central courtyard with a temple to Pazhayannur Bhagavathi.
  • Mural paintings, life-size portraits of Cochin kings, royal artifacts.
  • Materials: Laterite, wood, lime mortar, unique flooring (coconut shell, charcoal, lime, egg whites).

Civil Engineering & Materials

  • Foundation: Laterite plinth for flood protection.
  • Walls: Laterite, lime mortar.
  • Roof: Wooden truss, tiled for drainage.
  • Water Management: Central courtyard for rainwater collection.
  • Ventilation: Open corridors and courtyards for natural cooling.

Builders’ Thought Process

  • Community and royal focus: Designed for royal residence and administration.
  • Climate adaptation: High ceilings, cross-ventilation, and tiled roof.
  • Artistic expression: Mural paintings, carved wooden ceilings.

Modern Comparison

Feature Traditional (Laterite/Wood) Modern RCC/Steel
Durability 450+ years (with upkeep) 80–120 years
Maintenance Moderate Frequent

Unique Features

  • Impressive mural paintings of Hindu epics.
  • Life-size portraits of Cochin kings.
  • Unique flooring made from coconut shell, charcoal, lime, and egg whites.
  • Central temple for the royal deity.

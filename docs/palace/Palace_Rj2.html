City Palace, Udaipur

Regal Marvel on Lake Pichola, Rajasthan, India

Location

  • City: Udaipur, Udaipur District, Rajasthan, India
  • Coordinates: 24.5754° N, 73.6831° E

Dynasty

  • Sisodia Rajputs (16th century CE)

Deity

  • None (Royal palace)

Historical Background

Founded in 1559 by Maharana Udai Singh II, the City Palace is a sprawling complex expanded by successive Sisodia rulers. It overlooks Lake Pichola, blending Rajput and Mughal styles.
Source: Wikipedia

Building Materials & Properties

  • Sandstone: ~40–60 MPa strength, ~5–8% porosity
  • Marble: Decorative elements (~60–80 MPa strength)
  • Lime-Surkhi Mortar: Binder:aggregate ratio ~1:2, with organic additives
  • Properties: Sandstone is durable, suited for carvings; marble is aesthetic, low porosity

Comparison: Lime Masonry vs RCC

  • City Palace: Flexible, breathable, suited to semi-arid climates
  • RCC Structures: Stronger but less breathable, prone to moisture issues
  • Advantage: Lime mortar’s elasticity absorbs stress
Unique Features:
  • Mor Chowk: Peacock mosaics, a human-crafted masterpiece
  • Lake Pichola Integration: Scenic palace-lake harmony
  • Zenana Mahal, chhatris, mirror-work, and courtyards
  • Blend of Rajput and Mughal architecture

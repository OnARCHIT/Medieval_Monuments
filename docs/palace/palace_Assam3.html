Rang Ghar

#ahom #sportsarena #brick #assam #precolonialstadium #vaultedroof

Location & Period

Location: Sivasagar, Assam
Dynasty/Period: Ahom Dynasty, built in 1744–1750 CE
Commissioned by: King Pramatta Singha

History

  • Known as the “Colosseum of the East”, Rang Ghar is India’s oldest surviving amphitheater.
  • Built by Ahom ruler Pramatta Singha for royalty to view sports and festivals, especially buffalo fights during Bihu.
  • Reflects the cultural prosperity and leisure architecture of the late Ahom period.

Architecture

  • A two-storied oval pavilion with a roof resembling an inverted boat (possibly inspired by local thatched roofs).
  • Dimensions: ~10 m high, ~27 m long.
  • The upper floor had arched openings for viewing, with ornamental stucco on exterior walls.
  • Integrated semi-open galleries, stairways, and parapets for accessibility and visibility.

Civil Engineering & Materials

  • Made using burnt bricks and indigenous mortar (lime, molasses, and plant extracts like uradh dal).
  • Vaulted brick roof built with corbelled arch construction techniques.
  • Bricks placed in interlocking positions using compression principles, providing stability without steel.
  • Natural slope of roof and rainwater channels to withstand heavy monsoon.

Builders’ Thought Process

  • Designed not for religious or military purposes, but for royal leisure and public events.
  • Focused on visibility, comfort, and ventilation, which influenced the open-plan design.
  • Use of brick and mortar reflected locally available materials and growing Ahom mastery of masonry.
  • Adapted traditional bamboo/wooden pavilion forms into a permanent, monumental structure.

Modern Comparison

Feature Rang Ghar (Traditional) Modern RCC Stadiums
Structural System Vaulted brick + lime mortar RCC beams, slabs, and steel reinforcements
Roof Design Inverted boat-shaped vault Flat or domed with tensile roofing
Ventilation Open arches for cross breeze HVAC systems

Unique Features

  • Considered Asia’s oldest surviving sports pavilion.
  • Iconic inverted-boat vaulted roof, a rare innovation in medieval Indian civil design.
  • Blends indigenous aesthetics with pre-modern crowd infrastructure.
  • Evidence of cultural sophistication and non-religious architecture under the Ahoms.

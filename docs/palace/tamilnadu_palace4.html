Chettinad Mansions (Kanadukathan/Karaikudi)

#palace #chettinad #merchant #civilengineering

Location & Community

  • Location: Karaikudi, Sivaganga District
  • Built: 19th–early 20th centuries
  • Community: Chettiar merchant princes

History

  • Built by wealthy Chettiar merchants as symbols of prosperity and cosmopolitan taste.
  • Many are now heritage hotels or museums.

Architecture

  • 80,000+ sq ft mansions, rectangular plots, grand entrances, carved wooden doors, and panels.
  • Athangudi tiles, stained glass, Italian marble, Burmese teak, Belgian mirrors.
  • Central courtyards (Nalukettu), pillared halls, and open verandahs.

Civil Engineering & Materials

  • Materials: Brick, lime plaster, Burma teak, Athangudi tiles.
  • Walls: 1–1.5m thick for insulation.
  • Water Management: Rainwater harvesting, underground tanks.
  • Ventilation: High ceilings, courtyards, and cross-ventilation.

Builders’ Thought Process

  • Used imported and local materials for durability and status.
  • Designed for climate adaptation, social functions, and family gatherings.

Unique Features

Fatehgarh Sahib

#gurdwara #sikharchitecture #marble #memorial #civilengineering

Location & Period

Location: Fatehgarh Sahib District, Punjab
Dynasty/Period: Built and expanded post-1700s; current Gurudwara constructed in the 19th–20th century

History

  • Memorial shrine built at the site where Sahibzadas Zorawar Singh and Fateh Singh, sons of Guru Gobind Singh, were martyred in 1705 by the Mughals.
  • The site became sacred in Sikh memory and was developed over time into a major Gurdwara complex.
  • The main Gurdwara building was completed in the 19th century, with further renovations by Sikh rulers and modern SGPC.

Architecture

  • Multi-domed Gurdwara featuring traditional Sikh-style domes, arches, and chattris.
  • Includes the main shrine (Gurudwara Fatehgarh Sahib), Bhora Sahib (underground cell), and adjacent commemorative structures.
  • Surrounded by fort-like walls with prominent white marble cladding.
  • Interior has gold and fresco decoration, and the shrine faces east.

Civil Engineering & Materials

  • Built with brick masonry core and marble cladding for durability and sanctity.
  • Lime mortar used traditionally; later sections use cement mortar.
  • Domes constructed using reinforced traditional techniques.
  • Features raised platform, drainage systems, and arched supports for structural integrity.

Builders’ Thought Process

  • Marble chosen for purity and longevity, a common practice in Sikh sacred architecture.
  • Structure elevated for flood protection and prominence.
  • Architecture meant to reflect devotional sacrifice, with features that inspire reverence.
  • Blend of memorial symbolism and spiritual function.

Modern Comparison

Unique Features

  • Built directly on the site of martyrdom of the Sahibzadas.
  • Houses Bhora Sahib, an underground section where they were imprisoned.
  • A rare example of memorial merging with sacred space in Sikh architecture.

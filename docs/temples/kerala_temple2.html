Aranmula Parthasarathy Temple, Pathanamthitta

#temple #kerala #pathanamthitta #medieval #civilengineering

Location & Period

Location: Aranmula, Pathanamthitta District
Period: 12th–14th century
Deity: Krishna

History

  • Famous for its association with the Mahabharata and the annual Aranmula Boat Race.
  • Patronized by local rulers and the Travancore kings.
  • Classic example of Kerala temple architecture.

Architecture

  • Srikovil: Square sanctum with pyramidal roof.
  • Nalambalam: Quadrangular enclosure.
  • Namaskara Mandapam: Detached ritual hall.
  • Prakara: Large enclosure with sub-shrines and temple tank.

Civil Engineering & Materials

  • Foundation: Granite.
  • Superstructure: Laterite, wood, clay tiles.
  • Roof: Sloping, tiled, timber frame.
  • Water Management: Temple tank and wells.

Builders’ Thought Process

  • Sloping roofs and raised platforms for climate adaptation.
  • Proportionate design based on the garbhagriha module.
  • Community focus for large gatherings and festivals.

Modern Comparison

Feature Traditional (Wood/Laterite) Modern RCC/Steel
Durability 500+ years 80–120 years
Maintenance Moderate Frequent

Unique Features

  • Aranmula Boat Race: Famous annual event.
  • Mural paintings depicting scenes from the Mahabharata.
  • Unique rituals associated with the Pandavas.

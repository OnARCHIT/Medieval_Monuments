Gangaikonda Cholapuram Temple

#temple #chola #granite #UNESCO #civilengineering

Location & Period

Location: Gangaikonda Cholapuram
Dynasty: Chola
Builder: Rajendra Chola I
Built: 1035 CE
Deity: Shiva

History

  • Built to commemorate Rajendra Chola I’s northern conquests.
  • Served as the new imperial capital and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Architecture

  • 55m vimana, broader and more ornate than Thanjavur.
  • Lion-shaped well, temple tank, and elaborate sculptures.
  • Refined style—graceful rather than massive like Brihadeeswarar.

Civil Engineering & Materials

  • Granite and lime mortar construction.
  • Advanced water management through temple tank.
  • Foundation on compacted earth with wide base for stability.

Builders’ Thought Process

  • Granite for imperial grandeur and long-term endurance.
  • Designed to rival and refine Thanjavur’s monumentalism.

Somapura Mahavihara

UNESCO Buddhist Monastery, Paharpur, Bangladesh

Location

  • City: Paharpur, Naogaon, Rajshahi, Bangladesh

Dynasty

  • Pala Dynasty (8th–12th centuries CE)

Deity

  • Buddhist (Various Buddhist deities)

Historical Background

Built between 781–821 CE by Sree Dharma Pala of the Pala Dynasty, Somapura Mahavihara was a prominent Buddhist learning center from the 8th to 12th centuries. It influenced architectural styles in Tibet and Southeast Asia, notably Angkor Wat. The monastery declined after the 13th-century Muslim conquests and is now a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
Source: UNESCO

Building Materials & Properties

  • Brick: Locally sourced, burnt for durability, suited to Bengal’s humid climate
  • Terracotta: Decorative plaques, resistant to weathering
  • Wooden Members: Support chala-style roofing, add flexibility

Comparison: Brick/Terracotta vs RCC

  • Somapura: Brick and terracotta rely on compressive strength, thick walls, and robust foundations
  • RCC Structures: Allow thinner, taller forms and more seismic resilience
  • Advantage: Brick matches RCC’s compressive strength but lacks tensile and seismic performance
Unique Features:
  • Terracotta Plaques: Buddhist and daily life scenes
  • Cruciform Plan: Four-sided entry, spatial drama
  • Chala Roofing: Adapts to monsoon climate
  • UNESCO Status: Recognized for global influence

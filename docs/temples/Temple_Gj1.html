Sun Temple, Modhera

Solanki Sun Temple, Mehsana District, Gujarat, India

Location

  • City: Modhera, Mehsana District, Gujarat, India
  • Coordinates: 23.5833° N, 72.1333° E

Dynasty

  • Solanki Dynasty (11th century CE)

Deity

  • Surya (Sun God)

Historical Background

Constructed in 1026 CE by King Bhimdev I, the Sun Temple at Modhera is a Solanki architectural gem, dedicated to Surya. It reflects Gujarat’s prosperity and astronomical knowledge.
Source: Gujarat Tourism

Building Materials & Properties

  • Sandstone: ~40–60 MPa strength, ~5–8% porosity
  • Lime Mortar: Binder:aggregate ratio ~1:3, with organic additives
  • Properties: Sandstone is durable and suited for detailed carvings; lime mortar is breathable, ideal for semi-arid climates

Comparison: Lime Masonry vs RCC

  • Sun Temple: Flexible, suited to semi-arid climates
  • RCC Structures: Stronger but less breathable, prone to thermal stress
  • Advantage: Lime mortar’s elasticity absorbs stress
Unique Features:
  • Surya Kunda: Stepped tank aligned with sunrise, 108 shrines
  • Astronomical alignment: Equinox sunlight illuminates sanctum
  • Intricate carvings: Surya, deities, erotic motifs, and life cycle panels
  • Earthquake-resistant locking system, Maru-Gurjara style

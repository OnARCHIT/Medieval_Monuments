Ettumanoor Mahadeva Temple, Kottayam

#temple #kerala #kottayam #medieval #civilengineering

Location & Period

Location: Ettumanoor, Kottayam District
Period: 16th century (with earlier origins)
Deity: Shiva

History

  • One of the most important Shiva temples in Kerala, with ancient origins but rebuilt and expanded in the 16th century by local rulers.
  • Famous for its mural paintings and the annual Ezhara Ponnana festival.

Architecture

  • Srikovil: Square sanctum with a pyramidal roof.
  • Nalambalam: Quadrangular enclosure.
  • Namaskara Mandapam: Detached hall for rituals.
  • Prakara: Large enclosure with shrines for sub-deities and a temple tank.

Civil Engineering & Materials

  • Foundation: Granite.
  • Superstructure: Laterite, wood, and clay tiles.
  • Roof: Sloping, tiled, timber frame.
  • Water Management: Temple tank and wells.

Builders’ Thought Process

  • Sloping roofs and raised platforms for climate adaptation.
  • Designed for mural paintings and festivals.
  • Large enclosure for community gatherings and festivals.

Modern Comparison

Feature Traditional (Wood/Laterite) Modern RCC/Steel
Durability 500+ years 80–120 years
Maintenance Moderate Frequent

Unique Features

  • Mural paintings: Famous for its 16th-century murals.
  • Ezhara Ponnana: Annual festival with a procession of seven golden elephants.
  • Ancient origins: Rebuilt in the medieval period.

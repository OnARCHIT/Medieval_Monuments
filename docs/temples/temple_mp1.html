Historical Background

  • Location: Khajuraho, Chhatarpur, Madhya Pradesh, India (24.8520° N, 79.9210° E)
  • Dynasty: Chandela (9th–11th century CE)
  • Deity: Hindu and Jain (Shiva, Vishnu, Tirthankaras)
  • UNESCO World Heritage Site: Since 1986
“Khajuraho Temples are masterpieces of Nagara-style architecture, famous for their intricate sculptures and cosmic symbolism.”
– UNESCO

Architecture

  • Style: Nagara (North Indian), with curvilinear shikhara spires
  • Key Temples: Kandariya Mahadeva (Shiva), Lakshmana Temple (Vishnu)
  • Features: Elevated platforms, intricate sandstone carvings, spatial harmony per Manasara
  • Unique: Erotic sculptures (~10% of carvings), apsaras, deities, daily life scenes

Building Materials & Properties

Material Properties Reason for Use
Sandstone (Panna) Compressive strength 40–60 MPa, 5–8% porosity, carvable, durable Ideal for intricate sculptures and humid climate
Lime Mortar Non-hydraulic, breathable, binder:aggregate ~1:3, organic additives Flexibility, moisture regulation, longevity

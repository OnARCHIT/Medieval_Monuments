Historical Background

Built in the mid-13th century by King Narasimhadeva I of the Eastern Ganga Dynasty, the Konark Sun Temple is a UNESCO World Heritage Site renowned for its Kalinga architectural style. Designed as a colossal stone chariot for Surya, it reflects Odisha’s maritime trade and artistic patronage.
References: Wikipedia, Odisha Tourism

Architecture

  • Temple shaped like a chariot with 24 carved wheels (each nearly 12 feet in diameter) and seven horses.
  • Features a towering rekha deula (curvilinear spire) and jagamohan (audience hall).
  • Intricate khondalite stone carvings depict mythological scenes, celestial beings, and daily life.
  • Adheres to Kalinga and Manasara architectural principles.

Reference: UNESCO

Building Material Use & Properties

  • Khondalite Stone: Locally sourced, ~50–70 MPa compressive strength, ideal for intricate carvings.
  • Chlorite Stone: Used for doorframes, ~60–80 MPa strength, low porosity.
  • Lime Mortar: Non-hydraulic, binder:aggregate ~1:3, with organic additives (jaggery, rice husk).
  • Properties: Khondalite is durable and weather-resistant, lime mortar is breathable and flexible.

Reference: Ngoma, 2009

Builder Thought Process

King Narasimhadeva I aimed to symbolize divine movement and royal grandeur, using khondalite for its carvability and lime mortar for durability in Odisha’s humid coastal climate. The chariot design reflects Surya’s cosmic journey and aligns with Manasara for spiritual and aesthetic harmony.

Structural Design & Current Condition

  • Load-bearing khondalite masonry with corbelled arches and a deep foundation to counter coastal erosion.
  • Main deula (tower) collapsed in the 19th century; remaining structures stabilized by ASI using lime-based repairs.
  • Salt efflorescence and weathering remain ongoing conservation challenges.

Reference: INTACH Heritage at Risk Register

Comparison with RCC Structures

  • Konark Temple (Lime-Masonry): Flexible, breathable, suited to humid coastal conditions.
  • RCC Structures: Higher strength but rigid and prone to salt-induced corrosion in coastal areas.
  • Advantage: Lime mortar’s porosity prevents moisture retention, unlike RCC.

Unique Features

  • Chariot Design: 24 wheels and seven horses symbolize time and Surya’s journey.
  • Sundial Effect: Wheel shadows mark time, reflecting astronomical knowledge.

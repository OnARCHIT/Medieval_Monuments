Qila Mubarak

#fort #punjab #brick #sikharchitecture #civilengineering

Location & Period

Location: Patiala, Punjab
Dynasty/Period: Built in the 18th century by Baba Ala Singh (founder of Patiala State)

History

  • Constructed in 1763 CE by Baba Ala Singh, founder of the Patiala dynasty.
  • Served as the royal residence and military stronghold for the rulers of Patiala.
  • Expanded under Maharaja Amar Singh and Maharaja Rajinder Singh.
  • Declared a heritage monument and under restoration by INTACH and ASI.

Architecture

  • Fort-palace complex featuring Moti Mahal, Sheesh Mahal, Darbar Hall, and Ran Baas.
  • Mughal and Rajput influences blended with Punjabi-Sikh style.
  • Ornate frescoes, arches, and jharokhas (balconies).
  • Walled fort with large wooden gates.

Civil Engineering & Materials

  • Built with Lakhori bricks and lime surkhi mortar.
  • Wooden beams and brackets for roofing support.
  • Plaster surfaces with lime and natural pigments for murals.
  • Ventilated rooms with arched corridors and vaulted ceilings.
  • Foundations laid with rubble stone and lime for stability.

Unique Features

  • One of the only living forts in India housing multiple palaces inside.
  • Rich frescoes and mirror work inside Sheesh Mahal.
  • Unique fusion of Sikh, Rajput, and Mughal architecture.
  • Important cultural symbol of the Phulkian dynasty.

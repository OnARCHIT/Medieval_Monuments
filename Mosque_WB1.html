Adina Mosque, Pandua

Bengal Sultanate Marvel, Malda District, West Bengal

Location

  • City: Pandua, Malda, West Bengal, India

Dynasty

  • Bengal Sultanate (1373 CE)

Deity

  • Islamic (None)

Historical Background

Built in 1373 CE by Sultan Sikandar Shah, Adina Mosque is one of India’s largest mosques, symbolizing Bengal Sultanate’s power post-1204 Muslim conquest. It blends Islamic and Bengali architectural elements.
Source: Wikipedia

Building Materials & Properties

  • Brick: Fired for durability, supports vaults
  • Lime Mortar: Seals joints, resists humidity
  • Stone (Limited): Decorative, due to regional scarcity

Comparison: Brick/Lime vs RCC

  • Adina Mosque: Brick vaults require more columns, offer thermal insulation
  • RCC Structures: Allow larger spans, seismic resilience
  • Advantage: Mosque’s materials suit Bengal’s climate, but lack RCC’s tensile strength
Unique Features:
  • Bengali-Islamic Style: Combines domes with curved cornices
  • Vast Courtyard: Supports communal worship
  • Minimal Minarets: Reflects regional adaptation
  • Intricate brickwork and arched cloisters

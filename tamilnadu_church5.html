Our Lady of Ransom Church, Kanyakumari

#church #portuguese #neo-gothic #civilengineering

Location & Era

Location: Kanyakumari
Built: 15th–20th centuries (Portuguese, rebuilt 1914)
Denomination: Roman Catholic

History

  • Built by Portuguese, rebuilt in neo-Gothic style in 1914.
  • Major pilgrimage site for local fishermen.

Architecture

  • White neo-Gothic façade, triple towers, blue stained glass.
  • Large nave, side chapels, and bell tower.

Civil Engineering & Materials

  • Brick, lime mortar, and later cement.
  • Deep foundations for sandy coastal soil.

Builders’ Thought Process

  • Designed for visibility, capacity, and coastal resilience.

Modern Comparison

Feature Traditional (Brick/Lime) Modern RCC
Spires Brick, lime, stucco RCC, steel frame

Unique Features

  • Annual festival, blue glass windows.

Jagannath Temple, Ranchi

Kalinga Heritage in Jharkhand

Historical Background

  • Constructed in 1691 by King Ani Nath Shahdeo.
  • Mirrors the architectural style of the Jagannath Temple in Puri, Odisha.
  • Major pilgrimage site, especially during the annual Rath Yatra festival.
  • Represents Kalinga architectural influence and historic ties with Odisha.

Architecture

  • Kalinga architectural style with a curvilinear shikhara (tower) and chariot-shaped structure.
  • Complex includes garbhagriha (sanctum), jagamohana (assembly hall), and natamandapa (dance hall).
  • Intricate exterior carvings depict mythological scenes and deities.

Structural Design

  • Temple elevated on a plinth, accessed by a stepped approach.
  • Shikhara rises in curvilinear form, crowned with amalaka and kalasha.
  • Load-bearing stone walls support the tower; corbelled arches in the interior.

Materials Used & Properties

  • Stone: Local sandstone, durable and easy to carve.
  • Lime Mortar: Flexible, weather-resistant binder.
  • Wood: Used in beams, termite-treated.

RCC Comparison

  • Stone masonry relies on compressive strength, unlike RCC’s tensile reinforcement.
  • RCC is faster to construct and more earthquake-resistant.
  • Stone offers greater longevity and aesthetic value if maintained.
  • Stone is less flexible under seismic stress than RCC.
Stability Features:
  • Elevated plinth distributes weight and reduces soil settlement.
  • Thick stone walls ensure compressive strength and stability.
  • Curvilinear shikhara streamlines airflow, reducing wind load.
Unique Features:
  • Chariot-shaped design, symbolizing Lord Jagannath’s divine journey.
  • Annual Rath Yatra with a massive chariot pulled through Ranchi.

Sankagiri Fort, Salem

#fort #chola #vijayanagara #nayak #maratha #civilengineering

Location & Era

Location: Salem District
Built: 15th–18th centuries (Chola, Vijayanagara, Nayak, Maratha)

History

  • Built by the Cholas, later expanded by Vijayanagara, Nayak, and Maratha rulers.
  • Served as a regional administrative and military center.

Architecture

  • Covers 13 sq km with 17 km of walls, 12 major and 70 minor entrances.
  • Includes temples, pond, granary, watchtower, palace, and mosque.

Civil Engineering & Materials

  • Granite blocks and lime mortar construction.
  • Walls up to 18 ft high, with bastions, pond, and rainwater tanks.

Builders’ Thought Process

  • Utilized the natural hilltop for maximum defense.
  • Multi-level design for redundancy and strategic advantage.

Modern Comparison

Feature Traditional (Granite) Modern RCC
Wall Strength Massive, impenetrable RCC, less mass

Unique Features

  • Patchai Kovil (Chola-style Shiva temple).
  • Delhi Gate with iron spikes.

Palamu Forts (Nagpuri & Shahpur)

Chero Heritage of Jharkhand

Historical Background

  • Located in Palamu district, built in the 16th–17th centuries by the Chero dynasty.
  • Served as military strongholds during Mughal and post-Mughal periods, reflecting strategic importance.
  • Associated with Nagvanshi rulers and later Mughal interventions.

Architecture

  • Rajput-influenced military architecture with Mughal fort design elements.
  • Massive defensive walls, bastions, and gateways; Nagpuri Fort is larger and more complex.
  • Includes residential quarters, granaries, and temples within the complex.

Structural Design

  • Constructed on elevated terrain for strategic advantage; thick stone walls (3–5 meters) for defense.
  • Bastions at corners for reinforcement and vantage points.
  • Gateways with corbelled arches blend Hindu and Indo-Islamic techniques.

Materials Used & Properties

  • Sandstone: Locally sourced, high compressive strength, durable.
  • Lime Mortar: Binds stones, flexible, weather-resistant.
  • Brick: Used in interiors and minor fortifications, lighter but less durable than stone.

RCC Comparison

  • RCC fortifications offer greater tensile strength and artillery resistance.
  • Stone masonry is vulnerable to heavy bombardment, needs more maintenance.
  • Traditional forts are less adaptable to modern warfare but hold historic and aesthetic value.
Stability Features:
  • Thick walls and hilltop locations enhance defense and stability.
  • Deep foundations prevent settlement in laterite soil.
  • Bastions distribute lateral forces, improving siege resistance.
Unique Features:
  • Twin forts' strategic positioning (Nagpuri & Shahpur) allowed coordinated defense.
  • Intricate carvings on gateways blend Rajput and Mughal artistry.

Bhimbetka Rock Shelters

Prehistoric Rock Art, Raisen District, Madhya Pradesh, India

Location

  • City: Bhimbetka, Raisen District, Madhya Pradesh, India
  • Coordinates: 22.9380° N, 77.6120° E

Dynasty

  • Prehistoric (Paleolithic to Mesolithic, ~30,000–10,000 BCE)

Deity

  • None (Prehistoric rock art shelters)

Historical Background

The Bhimbetka Rock Shelters, dating to ~30,000 BCE, provide evidence of early human habitation in India. Discovered in 1957, they feature prehistoric rock paintings and were declared a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 2003.
Source: UNESCO

Building Materials & Properties

  • Sandstone: Natural rock formations (~40–60 MPa strength, ~5–8% porosity)
  • No Mortar: Natural shelters, no constructed elements
  • Pigments: Ochre, hematite, charcoal for paintings, applied directly to rock surfaces
  • Properties: Sandstone is durable and weather-resistant; pigments are mineral-based and fade-resistant

Comparison: Natural Rock vs RCC

  • Bhimbetka Shelters: No mortar, inherently stable due to geological formation
  • RCC Structures: Strong but artificial, requiring maintenance against weathering
  • Advantage: Natural sandstone’s permanence outlasts RCC’s lifespan
Unique Features:
  • Rock paintings depicting prehistoric life, dating back to ~30,000 BCE
  • Natural shelters: Early human dwellings without structural modifications
  • Boar Rock: Large mythical animal painting in deep red
  • Auditorium Cave: Cathedral-like natural shelter

Sivasagar Temples

#templecomplex #ahom #shaktism #shaivism #assam #bricktemple #tankarchitecture

Location & Period

Location: Sivasagar, Assam
Dynasty/Period: Ahom Dynasty, built in 1734 CE
Deity: Shiva (Shiva Dol), Vishnu (Vishnu Dol), Devi Durga (Devi Dol)

History

  • Commissioned by Queen Ambika, wife of Ahom King Siva Singha, in 1734 CE.
  • Located on the banks of the Sivasagar Tank (artificial lake), built earlier in the 18th century.
  • A representation of Ahom religious architecture, blending local traditions with pan-Indian temple styles.

Architecture

  • Temples follow octagonal and curvilinear designs, with tall shikharas (towers) reaching ~104 feet (Shiva Dol).
  • Use of pancharatha plan and rectilinear mandapas.
  • Placed adjacent to the Sivasagar Tank, an 80-acre water body designed for aesthetics and ritual needs.
  • High reliefs and sculptural panels depict gods, celestial beings, and mythological events.

Civil Engineering & Materials

  • Constructed using burnt bricks, finely carved and laid with lime-sura (lime and molasses) mortar.
  • Thick walls and arched interiors with decorative cornices.
  • Bases and tank edges made from stone blocks to resist erosion.
  • Design includes proper drainage, and water harvesting through the tank.

Builders’ Thought Process

  • Situated near the tank to blend religious and hydrological design.
  • Use of brick due to the absence of good quality stone in the region.
  • Represented Ahom royal patronage of Shaivism and Shaktism.
  • Aimed to create both a sacred and civic space—a water body plus temple complex.

Unique Features

  • Shiva Dol is one of the tallest brick temples in India.
  • Built beside the Sivasagar Tank, which remains full year-round.
  • Reflects Ahom architectural identity, influenced by both Indo-Aryan and indigenous styles.
  • A rare tripartite temple complex (Shaiva, Vaishnava, Shakta) built by a single royal patron.

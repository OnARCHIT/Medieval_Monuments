Purpose Mausoleum for Mumtaz Mahal (no deity)

Historical Background

The Taj Mahal, commissioned by Emperor Shah Jahan in 1632, was built as a mausoleum for his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal. Completed in 1653, it symbolizes Mughal opulence and love, blending Persian, Islamic, and Indian architectural elements. It was designated a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1983.
Reference: Wikipedia

Architecture

  • Symmetrical design, central dome (70 m high), and four minarets (40 m each).
  • Main mausoleum, mosque, guest house, and charbagh garden.
  • Makrana marble and intricate inlays reflect Persian influences; iwans and pishtaqs from Islamic tradition.

Reference: The Hindu, 2013

Building Material Use & Properties

  • Makrana Marble: White, fine-grained, low porosity, high durability (~70 MPa compressive strength).
  • Red Sandstone: Used for plinths and secondary structures, strong and carvable.
  • Lime-Surkhi Mortar: Binder-to-aggregate ~1:2, with surkhi (crushed brick), jaggery, and hemp fiber for strength and flexibility.
  • Properties: Marble is weather-resistant, lime mortar is breathable and compatible with stone masonry.

Reference: CE & CR, 2021

Builder Thought Process

Shah Jahan envisioned an eternal monument to love, prioritizing symmetry and visual splendor. Makrana marble was chosen for its beauty and durability, while lime-surkhi mortar ensured stability in Agra’s climate. Organic additives, as per traditional Indian practice, enhanced the structure’s longevity.

Structural Design & Current Condition

  • Load-bearing masonry with a central dome and thick supporting walls.
  • Minarets are slightly tilted outward for earthquake safety.
  • Well-preserved due to marble’s durability and ongoing ASI conservation; pollution causes some discoloration, addressed with lime-based repairs.

Reference: INTACH Heritage at Risk Register

Comparison with RCC Structures

  • Taj Mahal (Lime-Masonry): High breathability, low thermal expansion, compatible with stone, sustainable but labor-intensive.
  • RCC Structures: Higher compressive strength, but less breathable and prone to cracking in humid climates; require more maintenance in Agra’s environment.
  • Advantage: Lime masonry allows vapor permeability, reducing moisture damage compared to RCC’s impermeability.

Unique Features

  • Pietra Dura Inlay: Semi-precious stone inlays (lapis lazuli, jasper) on marble, forming floral and geometric patterns.
  • Symmetry: Perfect bilateral symmetry, mirrored in the reflection pool.
  • Acoustics: Dome interior amplifies sound, enhancing spiritual ambiance.

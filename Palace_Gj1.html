Laxmi Vilas Palace

Indo-Saracenic Royal Palace, Vadodara, Gujarat, India

Location

  • City: Vadodara, Vadodara District, Gujarat, India
  • Coordinates: 22.2980° N, 73.1940° E

Dynasty

  • Gaekwad Dynasty (1887 CE)

Deity

  • None (Royal palace)

Historical Background

Built in 1887 by Maharaja Sayajirao Gaekwad III and designed by British architect Major Charles Mant, Laxmi Vilas Palace is an Indo-Saracenic masterpiece reflecting colonial-era opulence and Gujarati patronage.
Source: Gujarat Tourism

Building Materials & Properties

  • Sandstone: ~40–60 MPa strength, ~5–8% porosity
  • Marble: Flooring and decor (~60–80 MPa)
  • Brick: Core structure (~10–20 MPa)
  • Steel: Framework (~250–400 MPa tensile strength)
  • Lime-Cement Mortar: Binder:aggregate ratio ~1:3
  • Properties: Marble is aesthetic and durable; steel enables large spans

Comparison: Steel-Masonry vs RCC

  • Laxmi Vilas Palace: Steel matches RCC’s strength; masonry is breathable
  • RCC Structures: Similar strength but less aesthetic versatility
  • Advantage: Steel’s tensile strength enables grand spans
Unique Features:
  • Indo-Saracenic style: Fusion of Mughal, Rajput, and Gothic
  • Darbar Hall: Stained glass and mosaic work, a human marvel
  • Golf course, stepwell, museum, and miniature railway
  • Golden stone façade glows at sunset and sunrise

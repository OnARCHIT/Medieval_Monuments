Pudukkottai Palace (Rajagopuram Palace)

#palace #tondaiman #pudukkottai #civilengineering

Location & Dynasty

Location: Pudukkottai
Built: 17th–18th centuries
Dynasty: Tondaiman

History

  • Seat of the Tondaiman rulers, who governed Pudukkottai as a princely state.
  • Now houses government offices and a museum.

Architecture

  • Square plan, granite and brick walls, bastions.
  • Royal residence, temples, granaries, and courtyards.

Civil Engineering & Materials

  • Materials: Granite, brick, lime mortar.
  • Walls: Thick, with bastions for artillery.
  • Water Management: Tanks and wells.

Builders’ Thought Process

  • Granite and brick for strength and local availability.
  • Designed for defense, administration, and ceremonial use.

Modern Comparison

Feature Traditional (Granite/Brick) Modern RCC
Wall Strength Durable, thick RCC, less mass

Unique Features

  • Royal residence, granaries, and temples.

Lokanarkavu Temple, Kozhikode

#temple #kerala #kozhikode #medieval #civilengineering

Location & Period

Location: Memunda, Kozhikode District
Period: 12th–14th century
Deity: Durga, Shiva, Vishnu

History

  • Complex of three shrines dedicated to Durga, Shiva, and Vishnu.
  • Associated with the legendary martial hero Thacholi Othenan and central to the ballads of North Kerala.
  • Major pilgrimage site for martial arts practitioners.

Architecture

  • Srikovil: Separate shrines for Durga, Shiva, and Vishnu.
  • Nalambalam: Quadrangular enclosure for each shrine.
  • Namaskara Mandapam: Detached hall for rituals.
  • Prakara: Large enclosure with shrines for sub-deities and a temple tank.

Civil Engineering & Materials

  • Foundation: Granite.
  • Superstructure: Laterite, wood, and clay tiles.
  • Roof: Sloping, tiled, timber frame.
  • Water Management: Temple tank and wells.

Builders’ Thought Process

  • Sloping roofs and raised platforms for climate adaptation.
  • Designed for martial arts and community gatherings.
  • Multi-deity worship reflecting the syncretic culture of North Kerala.

Modern Comparison

Feature Traditional (Wood/Laterite) Modern RCC/Steel
Durability 500+ years 80–120 years
Maintenance Moderate Frequent

Unique Features

  • Martial arts association: Central to North Kerala ballads.
  • Multi-deity complex: Rare in Kerala.
  • Annual festival: Attracts large crowds.

Sidi Saiyyed Mosque

Jali Marvel, Ahmedabad, Gujarat, India

Location

  • City: Ahmedabad, Ahmedabad District, Gujarat, India
  • Coordinates: 23.0276° N, 72.5807° E

Dynasty

  • Gujarat Sultanate (16th century CE)

Deity

  • None (Islamic mosque)

Historical Background

Built in 1573 by Sidi Saiyyed, a noble under the Gujarat Sultanate, the mosque is famous for its jali screens, reflecting Indo-Islamic architectural synthesis. It is part of Ahmedabad’s UNESCO World Heritage City (2017).
Source: UNESCO

Building Materials & Properties

  • Sandstone: ~40–60 MPa strength, ~5–8% porosity
  • Marble: Decorative elements (~60–80 MPa)
  • Lime-Surkhi Mortar: Binder:aggregate ratio ~1:2
  • Properties: Sandstone is durable, ideal for jali work; lime-surkhi mortar is breathable, suited for semi-arid climates

Comparison: Lime Masonry vs RCC

  • Sidi Saiyyed Mosque: Flexible, breathable, ideal for jali designs
  • RCC Structures: Stronger but less suited for intricate latticework
  • Advantage: Lime mortar’s porosity prevents cracking
Unique Features:
  • Tree of Life Jali: Iconic latticework, a human-crafted symbol of Ahmedabad
  • Indo-Islamic Synthesis: Blends Persian and Gujarati styles
  • Ten semi-circular jali screens, central prayer hall, and minarets

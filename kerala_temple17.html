Vadakkan Koyikkal Devi Temple, Puthiyavila

#temple #kerala #alappuzha #civilengineering

Location & Deity

Location: Puthiyavila, Kayamkulam, Alappuzha District
Deity: Sree Parvathy (Devi)

History

  • One of the most revered Devi temples in Alappuzha.
  • Known for unique rituals and as a center of worship for the local community.
  • The annual festival draws devotees from across Kerala.

Architecture

  • Srikovil: Main shrine dedicated to Sree Parvathy.
  • Nalambalam: Quadrangular enclosure around the main shrine.
  • Namaskara Mandapam: Prayer hall for devotees.
  • Balikkal Pura: Altar stone area for offerings.
  • Temple Pond: Used for ritual purification.

Civil Engineering & Materials

  • Foundation: Granite
  • Superstructure: Laterite, wood, clay tiles
  • Roof: Sloping, tiled, timber frame
  • Water Management: Pond and wells for ritual and practical use

Builders’ Thought Process

  • Designed for Kerala’s humid, monsoon climate.
  • Used local materials for durability and climate adaptation.
  • Raised platforms and sloping roofs for drainage and comfort.

Modern Comparison

Feature Traditional (Wood/Laterite) Modern RCC/Steel
Durability 500+ years 80–120 years
Maintenance Moderate Frequent

Unique Features

  • Renowned for its annual festival and unique rituals.
  • Multi-deity worship within the temple complex.

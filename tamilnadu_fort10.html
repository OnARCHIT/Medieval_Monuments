Dindigul Fort

#fort #nayak #granite #civilengineering

Location & Era

Location: Dindigul
Built: 1605 CE (Madurai Nayak)

History

  • Built by Madurai Nayak king Muthu Krishnappa Nayakar, later held by Mysore Wodeyars, Hyder Ali, and the British.
  • Controlled trade routes through the Palakkad Gap.

Architecture

  • Rock fort, 270m tall, 2.75km circumference.
  • Double walls, cannon mounts, stepwell, temple to Lord Rama.

Civil Engineering & Materials

  • Materials: Granite, lime mortar, brick.
  • Walls: Cemented for artillery resistance.
  • Water Management: Rainwater reservoirs using steep gradients, moat and drainage channels.
  • Ventilated godowns, sloped ceilings to prevent water seepage.

Builders’ Thought Process

  • Granite for artillery resistance and permanence.
  • Designed for defense, water resilience, and storage.

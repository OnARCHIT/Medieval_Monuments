Jagannath Temple, Puri

Puri, Odisha, India — Eastern Ganga Dynasty (12th century CE)

Location Puri, Puri District, Odisha, India
19.8047° N, 85.8175° E
Dynasty Eastern Ganga Dynasty (12th century CE)
Deity Jagannath (Form of Vishnu)

Historical Background

Constructed in the 12th century by King Anantavarman Chodaganga Deva, the Jagannath Temple is a major Hindu pilgrimage site and a part of the Char Dham Yatra. It is renowned for the annual Rath Yatra festival and is a prime example of Kalinga architecture.
References: Wikipedia, Odisha Tourism

Architecture

  • Towering rekha deula (214 ft), jagamohan, nat mandir, and bhog mandap.
  • Khondalite stone carvings of Vaishnavite motifs.
  • Complex enclosed by two walls: Meghanada Prachira and Kurma Prachira.
  • Follows Kalinga and Manasara architectural principles.

Building Material Use & Properties

  • Khondalite Stone: ~50–70 MPa strength, ~5–10% porosity, durable in coastal humidity.
  • Lime-Surkhi Mortar: Hydraulic, binder:aggregate ~1:2, with surkhi and organic additives (jaggery, black gram).
  • Properties: Breathable, flexible, resists salt damage.

Builder Thought Process

The Ganga rulers aimed to create a spiritual and cultural hub, using khondalite for intricate carvings and lime-surkhi mortar for durability in Puri’s coastal climate. The temple’s scale and festival infrastructure reflect royal devotion and social integration.

Structural Design & Current Condition

  • Load-bearing khondalite masonry with a curvilinear deula and deep foundations.
  • Double-walled complex enhances defense and stability.
  • Well-maintained due to continuous patronage; ASI and temple trust use lime-based repairs for weathering.

Comparison with RCC Structures

  • Jagannath Temple (Lime-Masonry): Flexible, breathable, resists coastal salt damage.
  • RCC Structures: Stronger but prone to corrosion in humid, saline environments.
  • Advantage: Lime mortar’s elasticity absorbs stress.

Unique Features

  • Rath Yatra: Annual chariot festival with massive wooden chariots.
  • Double-Walled Enclosure: Unique defensive and symbolic design.

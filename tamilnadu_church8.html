St. George’s Cathedral, Chennai

#church #anglican #british #civilengineering

Location & Era

Location: Cathedral Road, Chennai
Built: 1815–1816 (British)
Denomination: Anglican (Church of South India)

History

  • Built as the principal Anglican church of Madras Presidency.
  • Seat of the Bishop of Madras.

Architecture

  • Classical style with tall white spire, Doric columns, and portico.
  • Large nave, stained glass, memorials to British officials.

Civil Engineering & Materials

  • Brick, lime mortar, and stucco; deep foundation for spire.
  • Designed for tropical climate and British aesthetics.

Builders’ Thought Process

  • Built as a symbol of British power and faith.
  • Designed for grandeur and civic ceremonies.

Modern Comparison

Feature Traditional (Brick/Lime) Modern RCC
Spire Brick, lime, stucco RCC, steel frame

Unique Features

  • Memorials, stained glass, and British-era tombstones.

UNESCO Gothic Marvel, Mumbai, Maharashtra, India

Location

Dynasty

Deity

  • None (Railway station)

Historical Background

Designed in 1887 by human architect Frederick William Stevens, Chhatrapati Shivaji Terminus (CST) is a UNESCO World Heritage Site (2004), blending Victorian Gothic and Indo-Saracenic styles. It symbolizes Mumbai’s colonial trade hub status.
Source: UNESCO

Building Materials & Properties

  • Sandstone: Locally sourced (~40–60 MPa strength, ~5–8% porosity)
  • Brick: Core structure (~10–20 MPa strength)
  • Iron and Steel: Framework for roof and supports (~250–400 MPa tensile strength)
  • Lime-Cement Mortar: Binder:aggregate ratio ~1:3
  • Properties: Sandstone is durable, ideal for carvings; steel enables large spans

Comparison: Steel-Sandstone vs RCC

  • CST: Steel matches RCC’s strength; sandstone is breathable
  • RCC Structures: Similar strength but less aesthetic versatility
  • Advantage: Steel’s tensile strength enables large spans
Unique Features:
  • Indo-Saracenic style: Blend of Gothic, Mughal, and Indian elements
  • Functional Heritage: Active railway station with historical significance
  • Central dome, clock tower, and intricate stone carvings

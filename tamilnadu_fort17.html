Vellore Fort

#fort #vijayanagara #granite #civilengineering

Location & Era

Location: Vellore
Built: 1566 CE
Dynasty: Vijayanagara Empire (Chinna Bommi Reddy & Thimma Reddy Nayak)

History

  • Built as a strategic military base and administrative center for the Vijayanagara Empire.
  • Later controlled by Bijapur Sultanate, Marathas, Mughals, Nawabs of Arcot, and British.
  • Site of the 1806 Vellore Sepoy Mutiny.

Architecture

  • Massive granite walls, double ramparts, and wide moat (once filled with crocodiles).
  • Spread over 54 hectares (133 acres), with bastions, irregular projections, and a star-shaped plan.
  • Houses the Jalakanteshwara Temple (Vijayanagara style), a mosque, and St. John’s Church—making it a symbol of secularism.

Civil Engineering & Materials

  • Materials: Granite from Arcot and Chittoor, lime-surkhi mortar, brick, wood for gates.
  • Moat: Deep and wide, gravity-fed by the Palor River.
  • Walls: Huge double walls, wide enough for two carts to pass abreast.
  • Bastions: Projecting irregularly for crossfire defense.
  • Foundation: Built on a rocky outcrop for stability; escape tunnel rumored but not proven.
  • Water Management: Rainwater harvesting tanks inside; moat as flood defense.

Builders’ Thought Process

  • Granite for artillery resistance and permanence.
  • Multi-religious buildings for inclusivity and stability.
  • Layered defense and redundancy for siege survival.

Modern Comparison

Feature Traditional (Granite/Lime) Modern RCC
Wall Strength Massive granite Brittle under impact
Water Defense Moat, gravity-fed RCC, needs pumps
Maintenance Minimal Frequent repairs

Unique Features

  • Jalakanteshwara Temple’s ornate gopuram and carved pillars.
  • Crocodile moat, site of the 1806 Sepoy Mutiny.
  • Museum with artifacts from multiple dynasties.

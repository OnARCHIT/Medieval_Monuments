Howrah Bridge (Rabindra Setu) – Interactive Web Portal

Kolkata, India • Iconic Cantilever Bridge • Opened 1943

Historical Background

  • Location: Kolkata, West Bengal, India (22.5850° N, 88.3468° E)
  • Opened: 1943 (replacing the 1874 pontoon bridge)
  • Patronage: British Colonial Government, Indian Industry (Tata Steel)
  • Type: Balanced Cantilever Suspension Bridge
  • Significance: Symbol of modern Kolkata, engineering marvel, busiest cantilever bridge in the world
“Howrah Bridge stands as a testament to India’s industrial prowess and engineering ingenuity, connecting Kolkata and Howrah across the Hooghly River.”

Architecture & Design

  • Main Span: 1,500 ft (460 m) between towers
  • Deck Width: 71 ft (21.6 m), with 48 ft carriageway and two 7 ft footpaths
  • Towers: 280 ft (85 m) high
  • Construction: Riveted steel, no nuts or bolts
  • Foundations: Caissons sunk up to 31.41 m below riverbed

Materials & Properties

Material Properties Purpose
High-Tensile Steel (Tiscrom) Strength, flexibility, corrosion resistance Main structure (26,500 tons)
Concrete Compression strength, durability Approaches, surfacing
Rivets Durable, fatigue-resistant joints All connections (no nuts/bolts)

Comparison with RCC: Steel allows longer spans and flexibility, RCC is bulkier and better for shorter spans. Howrah Bridge’s steel design enables its iconic length and resilience.

Construction Timeline

  • 1862: First proposal for a bridge across Hooghly River.
  • 1874: Pontoon bridge opened.
  • 1936: Construction of new bridge begins.
  • 1942: Main span completed.
  • February 1943: Bridge opens to public.
  • 1965: Officially renamed Rabindra Setu.

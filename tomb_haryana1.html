Sheikh Chilli’s Tomb

#tomb #mughal #marble #civilengineering #sufism

Location & Period

Location: Thanesar, Kurukshetra, Haryana
Dynasty/Period: Mughal Period, 17th century (reign of Shah Jahan)

History

  • Tomb of Sheikh Chilli (Abd-ur-Rahim Abdul-Karim), a Sufi saint and spiritual mentor of Mughal prince Dara Shikoh.
  • Built in the mid-1600s, reflecting Mughal respect for saints and mystics.
  • The complex includes a madrasa (Islamic school), mosque, and garden.

Architecture

  • Octagonal tomb crowned with a double-dome structure.
  • Inspired by Persian-Mughal funerary design.
  • Symmetrically laid complex with a charbagh-style garden, domed mosque, and madrasa.
  • The dome sits on a high drum with chhatris (domed kiosks) at the corners.

Civil Engineering & Materials

  • Built using red sandstone, with inlay work in white marble.
  • Dome likely of double-shell structure—inner structural and outer decorative dome.
  • Foundation designed for load distribution over loose alluvial soil.
  • Lime mortar used as a bonding agent, with plastering techniques to seal joints.

Builders’ Thought Process

  • Constructed to honor a revered Sufi, making it both a tomb and pilgrimage site.
  • Octagonal form chosen for symbolism (eight-pointed Islamic star).
  • Materials selected for aesthetic contrast (red sandstone and white marble).
  • Integration of madrasa and mosque reflects holistic spiritual environment.

  • An example of Mughal-era spiritual architecture in Haryana.
  • Contains a rare double-dome tomb in this region.
  • Still houses a working madrasa, preserving Islamic educational traditions.

Ekambareswarar Temple, Kanchipuram

#temple #pallava #chola #vijayanagara #civilengineering

Location & Period

Location: Kanchipuram
Dynasty: Pallava, Chola, Vijayanagara
Deity: Shiva

History

  • Ancient origins with major expansions by Cholas and Vijayanagara kings.
  • One of the Pancha Bhoota Sthalams, representing the element Earth.

Architecture

  • 59m tall gopuram, a 1000-pillared hall, and an ancient mango tree.
  • Massive granite walls and a large temple tank.

Civil Engineering & Materials

  • Granite for structural strength, lime mortar for flexibility.
  • Designed for stability in foundation and superstructure.

Builders’ Thought Process

  • Granite used for permanence, mango tree added for spiritual symbolism.
  • Large columned halls built to host festivals and processions.

Modern Comparison

Feature Traditional Modern RCC
Hall Size Large, columned Larger with RCC, less ornate

Unique Features

  • Ancient mango tree and symbolism of Earth as one of five elements.

Champaner-Pavagadh Archaeological Park

UNESCO World Heritage, Panchmahal, Gujarat, India

Location

  • City: Champaner, Panchmahal District, Gujarat, India
  • Coordinates: 22.4833° N, 73.5333° E

Dynasty

  • Gujarat Sultanate (15th–16th century CE)

Deity

  • Multiple (Islamic mosques, Hindu/Jain temples)

Historical Background

Founded by Sultan Mahmud Begada in the 15th century, Champaner-Pavagadh is a UNESCO World Heritage Site (2004), featuring mosques, tombs, and hilltop temples. It reflects Gujarat’s pluralistic heritage.
Source: UNESCO

Building Materials & Properties

  • Sandstone: ~40–60 MPa strength, ~5–8% porosity
  • Marble: Temple elements (~60–80 MPa)
  • Lime-Surkhi Mortar: Binder:aggregate ratio ~1:2
  • Properties: Sandstone is durable, suited for arches; lime-surkhi mortar is breathable, ideal for semi-arid climates

Comparison: Lime Masonry vs RCC

  • Champaner-Pavagadh: Flexible, suited to semi-arid climates
  • RCC Structures: Stronger but less breathable, prone to cracking
  • Advantage: Lime mortar’s porosity prevents moisture damage
Unique Features:
  • Jami Masjid: Ornate Indo-Islamic architecture, 172 pillars, seven mihrabs, and minarets
  • Pluralistic Heritage: Hindu, Jain, and Islamic coexistence
  • Rainwater harvesting: Stepwells, tanks, and "city of thousand wells"
  • Hilltop fortifications, pleasure pavilions, and the Kalika Mata Temple
  • Helical stepwell and Sat Manzil watchtower
